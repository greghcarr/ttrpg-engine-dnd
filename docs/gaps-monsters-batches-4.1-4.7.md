**Metallic Wyrmling ladder, content-monsters-batch-4.7 (5):** Brass Dragon Wyrmling (CR 1), Copper Dragon Wyrmling (CR 1), Bronze Dragon Wyrmling (CR 2), Silver Dragon Wyrmling (CR 2), Gold Dragon Wyrmling (CR 3). Opens the Metallic Wyrmling tier below the existing Young + Adult Metallic ladder, mirroring slice 4.3 (Chromatic Wyrmlings) for the Metallic side. Every entry sourced verbatim from `references/srd-markdown/monsters-A-Z.md` on AC / HP / abilities / saves / damage immunities / senses / CR / XP / PB. Each ships a `breathWeapon` field consumed by the slice-140 primitive (wired-breath-weapon cohort moves from 32 to 37 — every dragon Wyrmling now has a wired breath). Brass + Copper share the new CR 1 floor for the Dragon type (the previous floor was Black / Green / White Wyrmling at CR 2), making them the two lowest-CR Wyrmlings and the only Dragons in the pack below CR 2. CR span 1 to 3 mirrors the Chromatic Wyrmling tier (CR 2 to 4) one CR step lower across the cohort. Breath geometry mirrors the Chromatic per-color split: Brass / Copper / Bronze line breaths (20 / 20 / 40 ft), Silver / Gold cone breaths (15 ft each). DCs scale 11 / 11 / 12 / 13 / 13; damage dice 4d6 fire / 4d8 acid / 3d10 lightning / 4d8 cold / 4d10 fire. Each Wyrmling carries its canonical elemental immunity (fire / acid / lightning / cold / fire). Silver Wyrmling's Rend deals **piercing** damage (every other Metallic Wyrmling's Rend deals slashing — the only Metallic Wyrmling whose primary attack is piercing rather than slashing; the higher Silver tiers revert to slashing). Bronze + Gold Wyrmlings ship Amphibious (narrative; consumer territory). All five ship `traits: []` (breath weapon lives on the dedicated field). Brass + Copper Wyrmlings lack Multiattack in RAW (a single Rend per Attack action, the only Multiattack-less Wyrmlings in the pack — every other Wyrmling, Chromatic or Metallic, makes two Rends per Attack); Bronze, Silver, Gold Wyrmlings have the standard two-Rend Multiattack. All five also carry a *secondary non-damaging breath* that folds into the slice-4.5 secondary-non-damaging-breath gap family (Brass Sleep, Bronze Repulsion, Copper Slowing, Gold Weakening, Silver Paralyzing) — same per-color signature as the Young / Adult tiers, with the Wyrmling DCs scaled down (DC 11 to DC 13). Bronze Wyrmling's Repulsion Breath uses the same 30-ft cone shape with a 30-ft straight push and Prone condition; the push distance is halved from the Adult tier (60 ft) but otherwise identical to the Young tier (30 ft straight + Prone), exercising the same forced-movement primitive gap. Silver Wyrmling's Paralyzing Breath introduces the consecutive-failures escalation shape (incapacitated then paralyzed) — same shape as the Young Silver Paralyzing Breath at one CR higher, identical mechanic with DCs scaled down. Gold Wyrmling's Weakening Breath uses a 15-ft cone (Young Gold's was 30-ft cone, Adult Gold's 60-ft cone) — same recurring-save Disadvantage-on-STR-D20-tests + 1d4 damage-roll-subtract rider (1d4 in the Wyrmling tier, 1d6 in Young + Adult — the per-tier scaling of the damage-roll-subtract die is the first numeric scale exercised in this gap family). Deferred under existing engine gaps: Multiattack on Bronze + Silver + Gold (Brass + Copper Wyrmlings have no Multiattack — narrative absence); Amphibious narrative on Bronze + Gold; Metallic Rend exercises the absence-of-elemental-damage-rider case (no elemental rider at Wyrmling tier, same as the Young Metallic tier — the elemental rider first appears at the Adult Metallic tier). Pack monster total moves from 141 to 146; the wired-breath-weapon cohort grows from 32 to 37.

**Adult Metallic Dragon tier-up, content-monsters-batch-4.6 (5):** Adult Brass Dragon (CR 13), Adult Copper Dragon (CR 14), Adult Bronze Dragon (CR 15), Adult Silver Dragon (CR 16), Adult Gold Dragon (CR 17). Mirror of slice 1.18 (Adult Chromatics) for the Metallic ladder. Every entry sourced verbatim from `references/srd-markdown/monsters-A-Z.md` on AC / HP / abilities / saves / damage immunities / senses / CR / XP / PB; non-lair stats only (lair-version HP, LR uses, and breath-DC bumps dropped per the single-statblock-variant constraint). Each ships a `breathWeapon` field consumed by the slice-140 primitive (wired-breath-weapon cohort moves from 27 to 32). Huge size; CR 13 to 17 (Adult Gold at CR 17 ties with Adult Red as the second-highest-CR Adult-tier statblock in the pack, behind only the Ancient Chromatic and Pit Fiend tier). PB +5 on Brass / Copper / Bronze / Silver; PB +6 on Gold (the only Adult Metallic at the higher PB tier, matching Adult Red). Each carries its canonical elemental immunity (fire for Brass + Gold, acid for Copper, lightning for Bronze, cold for Silver); Adult Bronze + Gold ship Amphibious as narrative. Breath geometry expands from the Young tier: Brass / Copper line breaths grow from 40 to 60 ft, Bronze line breath grows from 60 to 90 ft, Silver / Gold cone breaths grow from 30 to 60 ft. DCs scale 18 / 18 / 19 / 20 / 21; damage dice expand: 10d8 fire / 12d8 acid / 10d10 lightning / 12d8 cold / 12d10 fire. The cohort exercises every existing dragon-tier mechanic at once: Legendary Resistance 3/Day on all five (folds into the existing gap alongside Adult Chromatics + Aboleth), full Legendary Action menus (3 uses/round, drawing from Pounce + a per-color signature action — Brass Blazing Light / Bronze Guiding Light + Thunderclap area-save / Copper Giggling Magic d6-debuff + Mind Jolt / Silver Chill + Cold Gale area-save / Gold Banish + Guiding Light — folding into the existing Adult-Chromatic legendary-actions gap with two novel sub-mechanics: Bronze Thunderclap's "deafened until end of next turn" rider on a thunder-damage area save and Silver Cold Gale's combined damage + 30-ft forced push extends the Balor pull-and-prone gap further), innate Spellcasting on all five (each Adult Metallic carries an at-will + 1/Day-each spell list including a uniquely-Metallic Shapechange-into-beast-or-humanoid at-will entry that overlaps with the existing polymorph planner gap but with the additional flat-no-temp-HP / no-concentration qualifiers), and a secondary non-damaging breath on all five (Sleep / Slowing / Repulsion / Paralyzing / Weakening, scaled-up DCs from the Young tier) folds into the slice-4.5 secondary-non-damaging-breath gap family. The Multiattack on each Adult Metallic exercises a more elaborate "multiattack-with-options" sub-shape than the Young tier: each can replace one Rend with either a secondary-breath use or a Spellcasting use, doubling the option count. On-hit elemental damage rider on Rend ships at Adult tier (1d8 elemental damage on top of slashing for every Adult Metallic, in contrast to the Young Metallic's pure-slashing Rend), exercising the same on-hit-elemental-rider primitive family as the Chromatic Rends across all four tiers. Adult Copper Giggling Magic's 1d6 subtract-from-D20-test debuff is the first non-flat-modifier per-attack-roll-modifier in the pack (closest existing is Bardic Inspiration adding a die, but Giggling Magic subtracts a die — same primitive shape, opposite sign). Adult Gold Banish (Charisma save vs 3d6 force damage + Incapacitated + transported to demiplane until start of attacker's next turn, then returned) is a brand-new legendary-action shape composing the existing-condition (Incapacitated) primitive with a temporary-removal-from-encounter primitive that doesn't exist; folds into the existing Legendary Actions gap as a novel composite.

**Young Metallic Dragon opener, content-monsters-batch-4.5 (5):** Young Brass Dragon (CR 6), Young Copper Dragon (CR 7), Young Bronze Dragon (CR 8), Young Silver Dragon (CR 9), Young Gold Dragon (CR 10). Opens the Metallic Dragon ladder with the canonical Young tier, mirroring slice 1.17 (Young Chromatics) and giving the pack its first non-Chromatic dragons. Every entry sourced verbatim from `references/srd-markdown/monsters-A-Z.md` on AC / HP / abilities / saves / damage immunities / senses / CR / XP / PB. Each ships a `breathWeapon` field consumed by the slice-140 primitive (wired-breath-weapon cohort moves from 22 to 27). The cohort exercises the new "metallic alignment block" — three Lawful Good (Bronze, Silver, Gold) versus two Chaotic Good (Brass, Copper), the first cluster of Good-aligned Dragons in the pack (every prior dragon was Chaotic or Lawful Evil). Breath geometry mirrors the Chromatics with a different per-color mapping: Brass / Copper / Bronze carry line breaths (40 / 40 / 60 ft), Silver / Gold cone breaths (30 ft each). DCs scale 14 / 14 / 15 / 17 / 17; damage dice 11d6 fire / 9d8 acid / 9d10 lightning / 11d8 cold / 10d10 fire. Each Metallic carries its canonical elemental immunity (fire for Brass + Gold, acid for Copper, lightning for Bronze, cold for Silver). Bronze + Gold ship Amphibious (narrative). All five carry a *secondary non-damaging breath* in RAW (Brass Sleep Breath, Copper Slowing Breath, Bronze Repulsion Breath, Silver Paralyzing Breath, Gold Weakening Breath) — these are at-will, no recharge, and apply conditions / forced movement / debuffs rather than damage; they do not fit the slice-140 single-damage breath primitive and are deferred under a new gap entry (the secondary-non-damaging-breath family — eight composite shapes once Adult / Ancient Metallic tiers ship, plus the existing Vrock Stunning Screech + Sleep-poison Couatl-bite-style sub-family it overlaps with). Bronze Repulsion Breath adds a forced-30-or-40-ft push that overlaps with the Balor Flame Whip pull-and-prone gap (push variant) and the Wyrmling-era line-cone-breath geometry; Brass Sleep Breath and Silver Paralyzing Breath both exercise a "two-failure escalation" save shape (incapacitated on first failure, then unconscious / paralyzed on second), a tier-degree-of-failure save shape distinct from the existing margin-of-failure gap (Giant Centipede / Ghost Horrifying Visage), branching on *consecutive failures* rather than margin per roll — same primitive family, parameterized by failure-count vs failure-margin. Gold Weakening Breath introduces a STR-based-D20-tests disadvantage modifier that decays via per-turn-end saves with a 1-minute auto-success cap (composite of attack/ability-test disadvantage + recurring-save + duration cap, none of which compose for a monster action today). Deferred under existing engine gaps: Multiattack on all five (with the optional-secondary-breath-substitution variant where one or two attacks can be replaced by a secondary breath use — a new "multiattack-with-options" sub-shape on top of the existing Multiattack gap); on-hit elemental damage rider missing from Metallic Rend in RAW (Metallic Rend is pure slashing damage, no elemental rider, in contrast to every Chromatic Rend's elemental damage rider — exercises the absence-of-rider case cleanly); Amphibious narrative on Bronze + Gold; Metallic dragons have no Magic Resistance at this tier per RAW (it appears at Adult / Ancient — confirmed against `references/srd-markdown/monsters-A-Z.md`). Pack monster total moves from 131 to 136; the wired-breath-weapon cohort grows from 22 to 27.

**Ancient Chromatic Dragon ladder, content-monsters-batch-4.4 (5):** Ancient White Dragon (CR 20), Ancient Black Dragon (CR 21), Ancient Green Dragon (CR 22), Ancient Blue Dragon (CR 23), Ancient Red Dragon (CR 24). Completes the full chromatic dragon ladder across all three age tiers (Wyrmling + Young + Adult + Ancient, 20 statblocks). Every entry sourced verbatim from `references/srd-markdown/monsters-A-Z.md` on AC / HP / abilities / saves / damage immunities / senses / CR / XP / PB; non-lair stats only (lair-version HP, LR uses, and breath-DC bumps are dropped per the single-statblock-variant constraint). Each ships a `breathWeapon` field consumed by the slice-140 primitive (wired-breath-weapon cohort moves from 17 to 22). Cohort-uniform Gargantuan size; CR 20 to 24 (the new top of the pack — Ancient Red at CR 24 is the new highest-CR statblock, Ancient Blue at CR 23 the second-highest, displacing Solar (CR 21) from the top); proficiency bonus +6 (Ancient White) or +7 (Ancient Black / Green / Blue / Red — the first PB +7 statblocks in the pack, exercising the upper-end of the slice-130 saving-throw fast-path and the schema's PB range cap of 9). Breath geometry mirrors the lower-tier Chromatics: cone for Green / Red / White (90 ft), line for Black / Blue (90 ft / 120 ft). DCs scale: 22 / 22 / 22 / 23 / 24 by color; damage dice scale dramatically: 14d8 cold / 15d8 acid / 22d6 poison / 16d10 lightning / 26d6 fire (Ancient Red's 26d6 is the densest single-roll on a wired breath weapon and ~91 average damage on a fail). Each Ancient carries its canonical elemental immunity (cold / acid / poison / lightning / fire); Ancient Green additionally carries poisoned-condition immunity (same per-color pattern as Young / Adult / Wyrmling Green). Ancient Black + Green ship Amphibious (narrative); Ancient White ships Ice Walk (narrative). Deferred under existing engine gaps: Multiattack on all five; Demonic Restoration / Diabolical Restoration analogues do not apply to dragons (no death-respawn); on-hit elemental damage rider on Rend (2d8 acid for Black, 2d10 lightning for Blue, 3d6 poison for Green, 3d6 fire for Red, 2d6 cold for White) folds into the existing unconditional-elemental-rider-on-bite gap; Legendary Resistance 4/Day (the 4/Day variant now used by the Pit Fiend in batch 4.2 plus all five Ancients here — six total) folds into the existing per-day budget primitive gap; Legendary Actions (3 per round drawn from a per-statblock menu including Pounce / Tail Swipe / Cloud of Insects / Cloaked Flight / Sonic Boom / Mind Invasion / Noxious Miasma / Commanding Presence / Fiery Rays / Freezing Burst / Frightful Presence) folds into the existing Legendary Actions gap shared with the Aboleth + Adult Chromatics — the Ancient menu items add several novel sub-mechanics (Cloud of Insects' concentration-disadvantage rider; Noxious Miasma's AC-penalty-until-end-of-target-turn rider; Freezing Burst's speed-0-until-end-of-target-turn rider) that fold into the same on-hit-save-or-condition family already deferred; Spellcasting (Ancient Black / Blue / Green / Red carry full at-will + 1/Day-each spell lists per the innate-spellcasting envelope gap; Ancient White does not have Spellcasting in RAW, mirroring its Young / Adult tier — the only Chromatic without it) folds into the existing innate-spellcasting envelope gap; Frightful Presence as a Legendary Action option (Ancient White only — different from the Adult-Chromatic at-will-aura version) is a one-off shape: a legendary-action cast of the Fear spell rather than the Adult-Chromatic aura. The 24-hour-immunity stamp from the Adult-Chromatic Frightful Presence aura is gone in the Ancient White's version, simplifying that strand of the aura-trigger family.

**Upper Devil ladder, content-monsters-batch-4.2 (4):** Horned Devil (CR 11), Erinyes (CR 12), Ice Devil (CR 14), Pit Fiend (CR 20). Closes the SRD 5.2.1 devil ladder above Bone Devil (CR 9, batch 1.19) and brings the Fiend type to symmetric demon / devil coverage after batch 4.1 closed the Type I-VI demon ladder. Every entry sourced verbatim from `references/srd-markdown/monsters-A-Z.md` on AC / HP / abilities / saves / damage R/I/V / condition immunities / senses / CR / XP / PB. Cohort-uniform damage profile: cold resistance + fire / poison immunity + poisoned-condition immunity, with Ice Devil promoting cold from resistance to immunity (the only devil with full cold immunity, mirroring Balor's fire-immunity promotion in the demon ladder). All four wire Magic Resistance via `{kind: "GrantMagicResistance"}` (four new slice-131 users, bringing the cohort to 26). Pit Fiend at CR 20 is the new highest-CR statblock in the pack alongside Solar (CR 21 still tops); it ships PB +6 and is the second pack monster with Legendary Resistance after the batch-4.1 Balor (the Adult Chromatics + Aboleth share the same gap). Horned Devil exercises a darkvision-with-unimpeded-by-magical-darkness qualifier (carried as plain `darkvision: 150` since the magical-darkness predicate is consumer territory under the current senses model). Erinyes is the first pack monster with `persuasion` listed as a skill outside the angels. All four ship rich action sets deferred under existing engine gaps: Multiattack on all four; Diabolical Restoration is consumer-territory narrative; on-hit elemental damage riders on melee (Horned Devil Searing Fork 2d8 fire, Erinyes Withering Sword 2d10 necrotic, Ice Devil Ice Spear 3d6 cold + Tail 4d8 cold, Pit Fiend Bite + Devilish Claw 4d8 necrotic + Fiery Mace 4d6 force + 6d6 fire) fold into the existing unconditional-elemental-rider-on-bite shape; Horned Devil Hurl Flame is the first ranged-attack-with-elemental-damage on a deferred monster (5d8+4 fire at range 150 ft, plus a flammable-object-ignites narrative rider); Horned Devil Infernal Tail (DC 17 DEX save, fail = 1d8+6 necrotic + an "infernal wound" condition with recurring 3d6 hp-loss-at-turn-start until healed by magic or stanched by a DC 17 Wisdom (Medicine) check) shares shape with the existing Bearded Devil wound-no-heal rider but adds the recurring damage and the bystander-action-skill-check expiry; Ice Devil Ice Spear's on-hit movement-and-action-economy restriction (lose bonus action + reaction, speed -10, can move or take one action not both, until end of attacker's next turn) is a new mechanic-level deferral (composite of speed modifier + action-budget reduction + duration); Ice Devil Ice Wall (Recharge 6) casts the Wall of Ice spell as a monster action (folds into the spell-as-monster-action installer gap); Pit Fiend Bite poisoned-condition rider with hp-regain prevention + recurring 6d6 poison + per-turn-end save + 1-minute auto-success (composite of on-hit save-vs-poisoned + wound-no-heal + recurring damage + recurring save + duration-cap auto-success) is the densest composite in this batch and stacks several existing gap shapes; Pit Fiend Hellfire Spellcasting (Recharge 4-6, two Fireballs at L5, optionally swapping one for L7 Hold Monster or Wall of Fire) folds into spell-as-monster-action + recharge primitives, with the spell-from-monster casting context still needing to ship; Pit Fiend Fear Aura (20-ft emanation WIS DC 21 vs Frightened until start of target's next turn, with 24-hour per-target immunity stamp on success) extends the batch-4.1 Hezrou Stench / Balor Fire Aura recurring-aura family and adds a per-target immunity-stamp wrinkle that maps directly onto the Adult-Chromatic-Frightful-Presence 24-hour-immunity gap (Pit Fiend's aura is identical-shape to Frightful Presence with the extra save-on-failure-each-turn pathway dropped — it's an at-start-of-turn fire-and-forget instead of a recurring save); Pit Fiend Legendary Resistance (4/Day, the first 4/day variant; Balor and Aboleth + Adult Chromatics are 3/Day) folds into the existing gap with a small parameterization for the per-day budget. Erinyes Parry reaction folds into the existing Bandit Captain / Knight / Gladiator monster-reaction gap; Erinyes Magic Rope action (DC 16 STR save, fail = 4d6 force + Restrained until rope is destroyed or released, with a separate AC 20 / HP 90 magic-rope-as-object lifecycle) needs a per-statblock attached-item-as-stateful-entity primitive that doesn't exist today. The Pit Fiend at CR 20 + Erinyes at CR 12 also nudge the pack's "Legendary Resistance users" gap to six (Aboleth, Adult White / Black / Green / Blue / Red Dragons, Balor, Pit Fiend) and the Fear-Aura-style aura family is now wide enough (Hezrou Stench, Balor Fire Aura, Pit Fiend Fear Aura, Adult-Chromatic Frightful Presence) that the aura-trigger primitive becomes the single highest-leverage missing engine slice for unlocking deferred Fiend / Dragon mechanics.

**Mid-tier Demon ladder, content-monsters-batch-4.1 (6):** Vrock (CR 6), Hezrou (CR 8), Glabrezu (CR 9), Nalfeshnee (CR 13), Marilith (CR 16), Balor (CR 19). Closes the canonical SRD 5.2.1 Type I-VI demon ladder started by Dretch (CR 1/4) and Quasit (CR 1) in batch 1.7; every printed SRD 5.2.1 demon now ships at least once. Spans CR 6 to 19 in a single batch (the second-widest CR band yet; only batch 1.15 Celestials reach higher). Every entry carries Magic Resistance, folded as a `GrantMagicResistance` trait (six new wired users, bringing the slice-131 cohort from 16 to 22). Cold / fire / lightning damage resistance + poison damage immunity + poisoned-condition immunity are universal across the cohort; Balor promotes fire from resistance to immunity (the only demon with full fire immunity) and adds charmed-condition + frightened-condition immunity (the only demon with charmed-condition immunity outside the angels). Nalfeshnee carries frightened-condition immunity (alongside poisoned). Balor at CR 19 is the second-highest CR statblock in the pack (after Solar at 21), the first statblock with PB +6, and the first pack monster with Legendary Resistance outside the Adult Chromatic Dragons + Aboleth. Marilith introduces the highest single-target attack rate in the pack (six Pact Blade attacks per Multiattack). The RAW action sets all defer under existing gaps: Multiattack on all six; Demonic Restoration is consumer-territory narrative (revives in the Abyss when killed outside it); poison / necrotic / force damage riders on melee attacks (Vrock Shred 3d6 poison, Hezrou Rend 2d8 poison, Nalfeshnee Rend 2d10 force, Marilith Pact Blade 2d6 necrotic, Balor Flame Whip 5d6 fire + Lightning Blade 4d10 lightning) fold into the existing unconditional-elemental-rider-on-bite shape (Adult Chromatics, angels); Glabrezu Pincer auto-grapple + Marilith Constrict on-hit save-vs-grapple+restrained fold into existing on-hit auto-grapple; Glabrezu Pummel multi-target save-vs-half-damage against grappled creatures folds into the multi-target save shape (Otyugh Tentacle Slam); Glabrezu Spellcasting (Darkness / Detect Magic / Dispel Magic at-will, Confusion / Fly / Power Word Stun 1/day, DC 16) folds into the innate-spellcasting per-spell envelope gap (Couatl, Dryad, etc.); Nalfeshnee Teleport (120 ft, action) + Marilith Teleport (Recharge 5-6, 120 ft, bonus action) + Balor Teleport (60 ft, self or willing demon within 10 ft, bonus action) fold into the monster-teleport gap (Blink Dog); Vrock Spores (Recharge 6, 20-ft emanation CON DC 15 vs poisoned with recurring 1d10 poison + holy-water-ends qualifier) and Vrock Stunning Screech (1/Day, 20-ft emanation CON DC 15 vs 3d6 thunder + stunned, demons auto-succeed) and Balor Death Throes (on-death, 30-ft emanation DEX DC 20 vs 9d6 fire + 9d6 force) all need monster-action vocabulary plus the recharge / per-day / on-death-trigger primitives. Three genuinely-new mechanic deferrals surface in this batch: (1) Hezrou Stench aura (CON DC 16 at start of any creature's turn within a 10-ft emanation, fail = poisoned until start of its next turn) and Balor Fire Aura recurring (3d8 fire at end of bearer's turn to each creature in a 5-ft emanation) need an aura-trigger primitive that fires on every turn-start of every nearby creature or every turn-end of the bearer; (2) Balor Flame Whip pull-25-and-prone (Huge-or-smaller target pulled 25 ft straight + Prone) needs a forced-movement-by-attack primitive (closest analogue is the Charge / Trampling-Charge knockdown rider but the trigger source is the attacker's hit, not the target's failed save); (3) Balor Lightning Blade reaction-restriction (target can't take Reactions until start of Balor's next turn) needs a per-target reaction-budget zero rider with the attacker's-next-turn-start expiry; (4) Marilith Reactive (one Reaction on every turn of combat, not just the marilith's own) needs an extra-reaction-budget primitive scoped to per-turn rather than per-round. Balor's Legendary Resistance (3/Day) lands together with the existing Adult-Chromatic / Aboleth Legendary Resistance gap and is the only legendary-action-family entry in this batch (no Legendary Actions menu on these six).
