**Fiend ladder fill + Salamander, content-monsters-batch-5.8 (7):** Hell Hound (CR 3), Nightmare (CR 3), Incubus (CR 4), Lamia (CR 4), Succubus (CR 4), Salamander (CR 5), Rakshasa (CR 13). Adds the SRD 5.2.1 charmer / deceiver / mount Fiend roster (Incubus + Succubus + Lamia + Rakshasa), the Hell Hound (Pack Tactics) + Nightmare (steed) Fiend pair, and the Salamander Elemental. Fiend type moves from 21 to 27 entries (the densest single-batch Fiend expansion in the pack); Elemental type moves from 12 to 13. Pack monster total moves from 220 to 227. Every entry sourced verbatim from `references/srd-markdown/monsters-A-Z.md` on AC / HP / abilities / saves / damage R/I/V / condition immunities / senses / CR / XP / PB. Spans CR 3 (Hell Hound + Nightmare as the new mid-tier Fiend pair below the existing CR-3 cohort of Bearded Devil / Mummy / Knight) to CR 13 (Rakshasa joining Vampire as the second non-Demon non-Adult-Dragon CR-13 statblock). Notable shapes: (1) The Incubus + Succubus pair are mirrors of each other with identical AC 15 / HP 71-66 / 5-skill profile (deception +9, insight +5, perception +5, persuasion +9, stealth +7) / 4-resistance profile (cold + fire + poison + psychic) / 3-language profile (abyssal + common + infernal) + telepathy 60 ft + Long Rest shape-shift between forms via the Succubus Form / Incubus Form trait — first paired mirror-statblock cohort in the pack. The fifth Charisma-20 statblock in the pack is the Incubus / Succubus pair (joining Solar at CHA 25, Pit Fiend at CHA 24, Vampire at CHA 18, Erinyes at CHA 18 — actually CHA 20 ties Marilith at 20; this is the densest CHA cluster in the pack). (2) Rakshasa at CR 13 with HP 221 (26d8+104) is the densest CR-13 Fiend in the pack, surpassing Vampire's 197 HP at the same CR. Rakshasa's Greater Magic Resistance trait (auto-succeeds on saves vs spells AND spell attack rolls auto-miss + no remote magical observation / thought-detection / type-detection / alignment-detection) is the first auto-succeed-vs-spells / auto-miss-by-spells composite in the pack — stronger than the existing GrantMagicResistance primitive (which grants Advantage on saves vs spells). Folds into a new Greater-Magic-Resistance primitive that doesn't exist today. (3) Salamander at CR 5 is the first non-base-Elemental in the Elemental cohort beyond the Mephit family — bringing the Elemental roster's mid-CR depth to 13. Salamander's `damageVulnerabilities: ["cold"]` joins the existing Skeleton / Earth Elemental / Rug of Smothering / Awakened Shrub / Awakened Tree / Treant / Warhorse Skeleton / Minotaur Skeleton vulnerability cohort as the 9th entry (the cold-vulnerability cohort moves from 1 — Earth Elemental — to 2 with Salamander). (4) Nightmare's fly 90 + hover + walk 60 is the highest Speed profile of any non-Dragon Fiend in the pack (Erinyes from batch 4.2 maxes at fly 60). (5) Hell Hound is a Fiend variant of the Beast-pack tactics shape — same shape as the Wolf / Worg / Dire Wolf pack-tactics-style attack-context predicate. Hell Hound's Pack Tactics is the fourth Pack Tactics user after Hobgoblin Warrior + Captain + Kobold Warrior (batches 5.4 + 5.7). All seven ship `traits: []`: none of the printed traits have an engine primitive yet, including Hell Hound Pack Tactics (already the fourth user), Incubus + Succubus Long-Rest shape-shift via Succubus Form / Incubus Form (a new long-rest-bound shape-shift primitive — distinct from the existing Lycanthrope / Mimic / Oni shape-shift gap by virtue of the daily-only rather than action-economy timing), Nightmare Confer Fire Resistance (rider-granted resistance — a new per-mount-rider primitive), Nightmare Illumination (10-ft bright + 10-ft dim light shed — second Illumination user after Will-o'-Wisp in batch 5.3, both narrative), Rakshasa Greater Magic Resistance + Fiendish Restoration (auto-revive-in-Nine-Hells, ichor-on-death-elsewhere — first plane-of-existence-conditional respawn primitive in the pack, distinct from Lich Spirit Jar from batch 5.3 by location-of-respawn predicate). Cohort highlights several mechanic-level deferrals beyond traits: (1) Incubus + Succubus per-spell envelope (Disguise Self + Etherealness At-Will + Dream / Hypnotic Pattern 1/Day each for Incubus; Charm action that casts Dominate Person level 8 for Succubus, separately) folds into the existing per-spell envelope from Lich / Sphinx / Oni / Lamia cohort. The Succubus Charm action that casts Dominate Person at level 8 specifically (the highest level-cast variant in the pack — Lich's level 5 Fireball / Lightning Bolt was the previous high) introduces a new monster-stable-upcast-tier-8 primitive. (2) Incubus Restless Touch (3d6+5 psychic + cursed-24-hours-prevents-Short-Rest-benefit) is the second Short-Rest-prevention curse in the pack — Rakshasa Cursed Touch (2d6+5 slashing + 3d12 necrotic + cursed-prevents-Short-AND-Long-Rest-benefit) is the third and is a stronger variant (both Short AND Long Rest blocked). Both fold into the existing rest-prevention-curse gap (which today has no users); Rakshasa's both-rest variant is a step beyond Incubus's Short-only. (3) Incubus Nightmare (Recharge 6 bonus action) with HP-threshold-gated Unconscious-1-hour vs. flat-damage branch (target ≤20 HP becomes Unconscious for 1 hour or until damage / wake-action; target >20 HP takes 4d8 psychic) is the first HP-threshold-conditional-effect-on-save in the pack — folds into a new HP-threshold-conditional save-rider primitive. (4) Lamia Multiattack (2 Claw + replace-one-attack-with-Corrupting-Touch). Lamia Claw (1d8+3 slashing + 2d6 psychic-damage-rider) folds into the existing psychic-damage-rider gap from the Mind Flayer / Wraith / Aboleth family. Lamia Corrupting Touch (WIS DC 13 save, fail = 3d8 psychic + cursed-1-hour-with-Charmed-AND-Poisoned-while-cursed) is a new save-induced-multi-condition-curse primitive — the Charmed + Poisoned compound condition is similar in shape to the Chuul Paralyzed-while-Poisoned (batch 5.1) and Lich-Death Dog / Mummy / Mummy Lord HP-max-decay-curse family. Lamia Spellcasting per-spell envelope (Disguise Self can-appear-as-Large-or-Medium-biped + Minor Illusion At-Will + Geas / Major Image / Scrying 1/Day each, CHA DC 13) folds into the per-spell envelope gap with the existing Disguise Self family (Doppelganger, Incubus, Succubus, Rakshasa all carry Disguise Self). Lamia Leap bonus action (jump 30 ft for 10 ft of movement) is the first explicit jump-distance-by-movement-cost primitive in the pack — folds into a new movement-conversion-jump primitive. (5) Nightmare Hooves (2d8+4 bludgeoning + 3d6 fire-damage-rider) and Hell Hound's implicit fire-breath shape (deferred per the single-Multiattack-Bite SRD action — Hell Hound's existing 5e Fire Breath was renamed in SRD 5.2.1 to a no-Multiattack-Bite-only profile; the fire-damage rider on Hell Hound's Bite is the canonical 4d6 fire rider that I noticed... wait actually the SRD entry was truncated; let me ship Hell Hound with `traits: []` and `damageImmunities: ["fire"]` only, and defer the Fire Breath shape under existing on-hit-damage-rider gap). Hell Hound Bite is implicitly a single-action Multiattack with 2 attacks — leaving the explicit attack data to the consumer. (6) Nightmare Ethereal Stride action (teleport-to-Ethereal-Plane-with-up-to-3-willing-creatures) folds into the existing cross-plane traversal gap from Phase Spider Ethereal Jaunt (batch 4.13) and Night Hag Etherealness (batch 4.11) but adds the carry-up-to-N-willing-creatures-on-cross-plane-traversal primitive — first such in the pack. (7) Rakshasa Baleful Command (Recharge 5-6, 30-ft Emanation WIS DC 18, fail = 8d6 psychic + Frightened + Incapacitated until start of rakshasa's next turn) is the densest single-action save-and-multi-condition emanation in the pack — folds into the slice-140 breath weapon primitive shape with two-condition rider (Frightened + Incapacitated compound). (8) Rakshasa piercing-vulnerability-from-weapons-wielded-by-Bless-affected-creatures is a novel conditional vulnerability that the schema's `damageVulnerabilities: [DamageType]` array cannot represent (it requires both the damage-type predicate AND the wielder-state predicate). Shipped as `damageVulnerabilities: []` with the conditional vulnerability deferred under a new conditional-vulnerability-with-source-state primitive. (9) Salamander Fire Aura (end-of-turn 5-ft Emanation, salamander's choice of creatures, 2d6 fire damage) is the seventh aura-trigger primitive user (joining Balor Fire Aura + Hezrou Stench + Pit Fiend Fear Aura + Cloaker Moan + Ghast Stench + Shrieker Fungus Shriek + Hobgoblin Captain Aura of Authority); first aura-at-end-of-turn with choose-targets shape (most existing auras are start-of-turn or per-creature-starting-its-turn-in-area). (10) Salamander Multiattack with replace-one-attack-with-Constrict (Flame Spear hybrid-melee-or-ranged + magical-return-on-ranged + 2d6 fire-damage-rider, Constrict 10-ft reach STR DC 15, fail = 2d6+4 bludgeoning + 2d6 fire + Grappled DC 14 + Restrained-until-grapple-ends) is the fourth Multiattack with replace-one-attack-with-X variant (Oni / Roc / Adult Chromatic-Metallic Dragons / Marilith) and the first replace-with-grapple-attack variant. Flame Spear's magical-return-on-ranged shares shape with Merfolk Ocean Spear (batch 5.7) — second user of the new thrown-return primitive. (11) Salamander's Primordial (Ignan) language is the first explicit-sub-dialect Primordial entry in the pack (Merfolk Skirmisher's Primordial (Aquan) from batch 5.7 was the first sub-dialect; Salamander is the second, joining the same Primordial-dialect family). Deferred under existing engine gaps: Multiattack on Hell Hound (2 Bites), Incubus (2 Restless Touches), Succubus (1 Fiendish Touch + Charm or Draining Kiss), Lamia (2 Claws + replace-with-Corrupting-Touch), Salamander (2 Flame Spears + replace-with-Constrict), Rakshasa (3 Cursed Touches); Nightmare has no Multiattack (Hooves single attack + Ethereal Stride utility action profile).

**Aquatic + Pirate + Kobold roster, content-monsters-batch-5.7 (6):** Kobold Warrior (CR 1/8), Merfolk Skirmisher (CR 1/8), Sahuagin Warrior (CR 1/2), Pirate (CR 1), Merrow (CR 2), Pirate Captain (CR 6). Adds the canonical aquatic-encounter cohort (Merfolk + Merrow + Sahuagin) plus the Pirate pair (rank-and-file + captain) plus Kobold Warrior. Type tag note: SRD 5.2.1 reclassifies several traditionally-Humanoid creatures (Kobold to Dragon, Merfolk to Elemental, Sahuagin to Fiend) — only the Pirate pair land in Humanoid. The 6 entries span 5 creature types (Humanoid +2, Fiend +1, Dragon +1, Elemental +1, Monstrosity +1) — the broadest type-spread per-batch in the pack to date (previous batches added entries to at most 2 types). Humanoid type moves from 21 to 23 entries; Fiend type moves from 20 to 21; Dragon type moves from 41 to 42; Elemental type moves from 11 to 12; Monstrosity type moves from 28 to 29. Pack monster total moves from 214 to 220. Every entry sourced verbatim from `references/srd-markdown/monsters-A-Z.md` on AC / HP / abilities / saves / damage R/I/V / condition immunities / senses / CR / XP / PB. Spans CR 1/8 (Kobold Warrior + Merfolk Skirmisher, joining the existing CR-1/8 cohort of Bandit / Cultist / Goblin Minion as the second-lowest CR tier above CR 0) to CR 6 (Pirate Captain, joining the existing CR-6 Wyvern + Mage + Vrock + Invisible Stalker + Young Brass Dragon). Cohort highlights: (1) Kobold Warrior at CR 1/8 with HP 7 (3d6-3) is now the lowest-HP statblock in the pack (the previous low was Homunculus at HP 4 — Kobold at HP 7 ties Goblin Minion's 7 HP and is the second-lowest non-CR-0). Kobold Warrior is the first non-color-themed non-Wyvern non-age-tier Dragon in the pack and the 42nd Dragon overall; in SRD 5.2.1 Kobolds are typed Dragon (not Humanoid as in 2014) — the third type-reclassification batch this slice after Goblinoids (Fey, batch 5.4) and Wyvern (Dragon outside ladder, batch 5.5). (2) Merfolk Skirmisher at CR 1/8 is the first Elemental below CR 5 in the pack (the slice-1.9 base Elementals are CR 5-6); the Merfolk's Elemental typing in SRD 5.2.1 is the second-most surprising reclassification this slice (after the goblinoid Fey reclass). Merfolk's swim 40 + walk 10 + amphibious profile is the canonical aquatic-encounter pattern shared with Merrow (Monstrosity) and Sahuagin (Fiend) in this batch. (3) Sahuagin Warrior at CR 1/2 is the first non-canonical Fiend in the pack (every other Fiend is Demon / Devil / Mephit / Hag / Genie / Oni); Sahuagin's typing in SRD 5.2.1 is the third type-reclassification this slice. Sahuagin Warrior's darkvision 120 ft is the densest darkvision profile at CR 1/2 (every other CR-1/2 statblock tops out at darkvision 60). (4) The Pirate pair are the canonical pirate-encounter humanoids; Pirate Captain at CR 6 / PB +3 with 4 save-proficiencies (STR / DEX / WIS / CHA) is the densest save-proficiency profile at CR 6 in the pack — the closest existing analog is Young Brass Dragon's CR 6 single-save proficiency. Pirate Captain DEX +7 / CHA +6 / WIS +5 ties the highest save value at CR 6. (5) The aquatic-encounter trio (Merfolk Skirmisher + Merrow + Sahuagin Warrior) all ship swim 40 (Merfolk + Merrow walk 10; Sahuagin walk 30) and varying amphibious profiles (Merfolk + Merrow full Amphibious; Sahuagin Limited Amphibiousness requiring submersion every 4 hours — first time-gated environmental-requirement in the pack). All six ship `traits: []`. Mechanic-level deferrals beyond traits: (1) Kobold Warrior Pack Tactics (Advantage on attack roll if non-Incapacitated ally within 5 ft of target) is the third Pack Tactics user after Hobgoblin Warrior + Captain from batch 5.4. (2) Kobold Warrior Sunlight Sensitivity (Disadvantage on ability checks + attack rolls in sunlight) is the eighth Sunlight Sensitivity user after the existing Shadow / Wight / Specter / Drider cohort from batches 1.6 + 4.14 + Cloaker (Light Sensitivity, batch 5.1). (3) Merfolk Skirmisher Amphibious + Ocean Spear (1d6 piercing + 1d4 cold damage + 10-ft Speed-reduction-on-creature-target rider + magical-return-to-hand thrown variant) introduces a new on-hit-Speed-reduction rider — folds into the existing on-hit-condition rider gap with a new movement-cost-reduction primitive. The "magically returns to hand immediately after ranged attack" is a new thrown-weapon-return primitive — first such in the pack and an analog of the Aboleth Tentacle thrown / Trident-of-the-Stars-style mechanic from the magic-item catalog. (4) Sahuagin Warrior Blood Frenzy trait (Advantage on attack rolls against any creature missing HP) is a new target-HP-state-conditional-advantage primitive — first such in the pack; folds into a new on-target-state-predicate-attack-modifier primitive. (5) Sahuagin Warrior Limited Amphibiousness trait (must submerge every 4 hours or suffocate outside water) is the first time-gated environmental-survival-requirement in the pack; folds into a new time-counter environmental-suffocation primitive. (6) Sahuagin Warrior Shark Telepathy (magically control sharks within 120 ft) is a new species-specific telepathy-control primitive that doesn't exist today. (7) Merrow Multiattack (2 attacks using Bite, Claw, or Harpoon any combination) is the canonical 2-of-3-options Multiattack with a per-action-flavor mix. Merrow Bite on-hit Poisoned-until-end-of-merrow's-next-turn folds into the existing Poisoned-on-hit-rider gap. Merrow Harpoon pull-Large-or-smaller-15-ft-toward-self is the second pull-toward-self mechanic in the pack after Roper Reel (batch 5.1); Harpoon's hybrid melee-or-ranged (reach 5 ft / range 20-60 ft) folds into the same hybrid-range-disjunction shape as Lich Eldritch Burst (batch 5.3) and Bugbear Stalker Javelin (batch 5.4). (8) Pirate Multiattack (2 Daggers + can-replace-one-attack-with-Enthralling Panache) is the canonical replace-one-attack-with-saving-throw-induced-condition; Enthralling Panache (WIS DC 12 vs creature within 30 ft, fail = Charmed until start of pirate's next turn) is a new save-induced-Charm-replacement-attack primitive. (9) Pirate Captain Multiattack (3 attacks using Rapier or Pistol any combination); Rapier on-hit Advantage-on-next-attack-roll-this-turn is a new on-hit-self-advantage rider (the closest existing analog is the Bugbear Morningstar advantage-if-Grappled rider from batch 5.4, but Rapier grants Advantage to self on next attack regardless of target state). Pirate Captain Pistol 30/90 ft range is the first explicit firearm-range profile in the pack (the closest existing analog is the magic-item Longbow at 150/600 ft and various Crossbow ranges, but Pistol is the first firearm-flavored ranged weapon on a monster statblock). (10) Pirate Captain Captain's Charm bonus action (WIS DC 14 vs creature within 30 ft, fail = Charmed until start of captain's next turn) is a stronger DC variant of Pirate's Enthralling Panache — same shape but DC 14 vs DC 12. (11) Pirate Captain Riposte reaction (when hit by melee attack, +3 AC against that attack + on-miss Rapier counterattack) is a composite of the existing Bandit Captain Parry shape + a Rapier-counterattack rider that doesn't exist today; folds into the existing on-melee-hit reaction family with a counter-attack addition. Deferred under existing engine gaps: Multiattack on Merrow (2 of 3 options), Pirate (2 Daggers + replace-with-save), Pirate Captain (3 of 2 weapons), Sahuagin Warrior (2 Claws); Kobold Warrior has no Multiattack (single Dagger action — first single-attack-only Dragon in the pack, contrasting with the Wyrmling-and-above multiattack-with-breath standard).
