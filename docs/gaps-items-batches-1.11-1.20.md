# Magic item gaps catalog (part B: items batches 1.11-1.20)

Per-item catalog of starter-pack items that ship as stubs versus what's mechanically wired. Extracted from [starter-pack-gaps.md](starter-pack-gaps.md) in slice 249.

This part covers the deferred mechanical wirings introduced by items batches 1.11-1.20: the rod / staff / wand cohort, potions and oils, spell scrolls, Ioun Stones, rings, the Giant Strength tier extension, robes and outer garments, cloaks and small-slot accessories, charges-bearing rings, and the charges-bearing wand expansion.

For pack inventory + batches 1.1-1.10, see [gaps-items-batches-1.1-1.10.md](gaps-items-batches-1.1-1.10.md). For the priority queue and "Future engine slices" backlog, see [starter-pack-gaps.md](starter-pack-gaps.md).

- **Items-batch-1.11 rod / staff / wand cohort** (the charges shape is in-pack; per-item action wiring routes through the same `UseItem` planner deferred for batch 1.7). All eight items in this batch are charges-bearing per RAW. Cross-cutting blocker is the `UseItem` planner from batch 1.7's deferral: walks the activated item's effect array, consumes the appropriate charges, and routes resulting effects (spell-grant casts, damage rolls, condition applications) through the existing planner surface. Per-item shapes once `UseItem` lands:
  - *Rod of the Pact Keeper +1*: composite passive plus active. The passive arm (+1 to warlock spell attack rolls and DC) wires once magic-item passive effects project to the wearer's stack (the magic-item-effect-projection pass already named for ~20 wondrous items) plus a "warlock spell scope" predicate the engine doesn't currently carry. The active arm (regain one expended warlock spell slot, 1/day) is the same `RegainSpellSlot` primitive Pearl of Power named in batch 1.7, with the additional constraint that the slot must come from the warlock class's slot pool specifically (multiclassed warlocks need the engine to disambiguate). The +2 / +3 variants compose with the same primitives at higher numeric values.
  - *Staff of the Adder*: bonus-action transformation into a giant constrictor snake. Same `SummonCreature` shape Hat of Vermin's batch-1.8 entry waited on, but with a fixed creature ID (giant constrictor snake from the monsters lane) and an item-bound lifecycle (the snake reverts to staff form when it takes damage). Once `UseItem` and `SummonCreature` land, the staff wires as a 1-charge-per-dawn invocation of the summon. The "reverts on damage" arm wants a damage-triggered despawn hook on the summon, which would generalize to other transformation items.
  - *Staff of Charming*: at-will spell casting via charges. Casts Charm Person at 1 charge (or save DC override 17 for the staff). Same `ItemSpellGrant` primitive Hat of Disguise and Helm of Telepathy named in batch 1.2, plus a per-cast save-DC-override hook the existing spell vocabulary doesn't carry (the spell's DC comes from the caster, not the item; the staff dictates DC 17 regardless of wielder). Two RAW spell modes (Charm Person and Command), each at 1 charge. Plus an absorption sub-shape: when targeted by a charm or command, the staff can absorb the incoming spell as a reaction and recover a charge equal to the absorbed spell's level. Absorption is its own primitive, distinct from the magic-missile-immunity sub-shape Brooch of Shielding wanted.
  - *Staff of Healing*: at-will Cure Wounds / Lesser Restoration / Mass Cure Wounds via charges (1 / 2 / 5 charges respectively). Same `ItemSpellGrant` primitive Staff of Charming wants, with the level-spend lookup choosing which spell the wielder is casting. The healing-output values use the staff's progression (e.g. Cure Wounds at 2d8+2 regardless of wielder spell level) rather than the wielder's slot level; needs an item-level-progression override that's distinct from the wielder's spell-slot choice.
  - *Wand of Web*: 1-charge-per-cast Web spell at save DC 15. Pure `ItemSpellGrant` once it lands with the staff-style fixed save DC.
  - *Wand of Magic Detection*: 1-charge-per-cast Detect Magic spell. Same shape as Wand of Web; this one is the first charges-bearing wand in the pack without attunement.
  - *Wand of Fireballs*: variable-charge spell scaling. 1 charge casts Fireball at 3rd level, with 1 additional charge per level above that (up to 7th-level Fireball if all charges are spent). Same `ItemSpellGrant` primitive plus a variable-charges-per-cast extension the simpler wands don't need (Wand of Magic Missiles ships with the simpler "1 charge per cast plus 1 per slot level above 1st" but the engine doesn't yet enforce this either).
  - *Wand of Wonder*: random-effect-table primitive. RAW: roll d100 on a 90+ entry table to determine what happens (each entry naming a spell, area effect, or narrative outcome). Same shape as Bag of Beans and Deck of Many Things deferred in earlier batches; all three wait on a `RollOnRandomTable { tableId }` primitive plus a content-pack table-of-effects shape that the magic-item schema doesn't currently model. The "table-of-effects" shape generalizes to wild-magic surges and other random-result mechanics across content yet to ship.
- **Items-batch-1.12 potions and oils** (route through the `ConsumeItem` planner deferred for batch 1.3's potion set; all eight ship as `onConsume: []` with narrative descriptions). Cross-cutting blocker is unchanged from batch 1.3: a `ConsumeItem` planner walks the consumable's `onConsume` array on activation, fires each Effect against the consumer, and emits the appropriate events. Per-potion variant shapes the planner would compose:
  - *Potion of Water Breathing*: `ApplyCondition` with a 1-hour duration carrying the breathlessness marker effect Necklace of Adaptation's batch-1.2 deferral named. Once both `ConsumeItem` and the breathlessness marker land, this potion wires as a single composition.
  - *Potion of Fire Breath*: composite consume-then-charges. The drink installs a 3-charge / 1-hour-duration condition on the consumer; each charge spend triggers a bonus-action breath-weapon attack (4d6 fire damage, DC 13 DEX save for half, 30-ft single-target range). Needs the `ConsumeItem` planner plus a "condition-with-internal-charges" extension of the existing condition shape (no precedent in the wired catalog — most conditions count down rounds or minutes, not uses). Plus a breath-weapon attack primitive scoped to a single target (the engine has cone / line / sphere area shapes from `AreaEffectSpell` but not "ranged single-target attack rooted in the user's mouth").
  - *Potion of Hill Giant Strength*: 1-hour `OverrideAbilityScore { ability: 'STR', value: 21 }` condition (the primitive batch 1.2 named for the Belt of Hill Giant Strength). Once both `ConsumeItem` and `OverrideAbilityScore` land, the potion and the belt share the same wiring at the same numeric value. The remaining giant-strength tier (Stone 23 / Frost 23 / Fire 25 / Cloud 27 / Storm 29) ships in a future batch under the same primitive at different `value` targets.
  - *Oil of Slipperiness*: dual-target consumable. Applied to a creature, grants the effect of the Freedom of Movement spell as a `ApplyCondition` with 8-hour duration carrying the spell's effect set (Freedom of Movement is currently schema-only). Applied to a surface, creates a difficult-terrain zone with a DC 10 DEX-save-vs-prone trigger for any creature entering it. Needs `ConsumeItem` plus a target-selection branch (the consume planner consults a `consumeChoice` field to pick which arm fires) plus the Freedom of Movement spell wiring plus a difficult-terrain-zone primitive that doesn't exist yet (the engine has area-effect spells but no persistent passive difficult-terrain markers on a tile or square).
  - *Potion of Invisibility*: `ApplyCondition` with the existing Invisible condition for 1 hour, with the "ends on attack or cast a spell" trigger from the Invisibility spell. Once `ConsumeItem` lands, this wires by composition: the Invisible condition vocabulary is in-pack, and the spell-ends-on-attack hook exists in the existing Invisibility spell wiring.
  - *Potion of Flying*: 1-hour `ApplyCondition` carrying `ModifySpeed { mode: 'fly', op: 'set' }` at a value equal to the consumer's walking speed at consume time. The `ModifySpeed { mode: 'fly' }` primitive is shipped (slice 77); the "value equal to walking speed at consume time" arm needs the consume planner to read the consumer's walking-speed derivation and bake it into the condition's effect at consume time. Plus the "falls if still aloft when the duration ends" arm is consumer-side / DM-discretion.
  - *Potion of Speed*: `ApplyCondition` for 1 minute carrying the Haste spell's effect set (additional action, +2 AC, advantage on DEX saves, doubled speed) without the concentration requirement. Haste is currently schema-only at L3 in the spell catalog; once it wires plus `ConsumeItem` lands, the potion composes with a single deviation (the concentration arm is suppressed for the potion variant). Plus the post-effect "lethargic for 1 minute, can't move or take actions" arm needs the engine to chain a follow-up condition onto a condition's expiry (no precedent in the wired catalog).
  - *Oil of Sharpness*: weapon-targeted consumable rather than creature-targeted. Applied to a piercing or slashing weapon (or up to 5 pieces of ammunition), grants +3 to attack and damage rolls and counts as magical for 1 hour. Needs `ConsumeItem` to route into the weapon-instance buff shape (slice 76's `ItemInstance.temporaryBuff` already models a temporary attack-and-damage bonus applied via a spell-tag; the oil's application path is the new wiring). Stacks with the batch-1.5 weapon templates exactly as the +N templates would expect.
- **Items-batch-1.13 spell scrolls** (fills the previously-empty scroll category; eight reference entries spanning cantrip through 9th level across all five non-artifact rarities). All eight ship as `itemKind: 'consumable'` with `onConsume: []` and a narrative description that names the underlying spell, the scroll-fixed save DC / spell attack bonus, and the non-class-list read-check DC per RAW. The scroll category is currently a sub-pattern of `consumable` rather than a distinct `itemKind: 'scroll'` discriminator (the schema would need extension to model scrolls as a first-class category with explicit `spellId`, `castLevel`, `saveDC`, `attackBonus`, and `readCheckDC` fields). Wiring per arm:
  - *Cross-cutting blocker* (same as the batch-1.3 and batch-1.12 potions): a `ConsumeItem` planner walks the consumable's `onConsume` array and routes resulting effects. Scrolls compose the planner with a fixed-DC `ItemSpellGrant` (the staff-of-charming variant batch 1.11 named, where the spell's save DC and attack bonus come from the item rather than the wielder). Single-use semantics: the planner deletes or marks-spent the consumable instance after a successful cast.
  - *Class-list gating arm*: RAW says scrolls on the consumer's class list cast as a normal spell; off-list casters must succeed on a DC `10 + spellLevel` INT or WIS check (consumer choice) to cast at all. Failure destroys the scroll without effect for level-1+ scrolls; cantrip scrolls just don't fire. Needs an "is this spell on the consumer's class list" predicate the engine doesn't currently expose (the class schemas carry per-class spell lists, but no derive helper unifies them into a "consumer can natively cast `spellId`" query) plus an ability-check resolution path the consume planner can branch on.
  - *Per-level scaling*: scroll save DC and attack bonus scale with spell level (DC 13 / +5 for L0–L2, DC 15 / +7 for L3–L4, DC 17 / +9 for L5–L6, DC 18 / +10 for L7–L8, DC 19 / +11 for L9). The eight entries in this batch ship the scroll-side numbers in the description; once the schema gains explicit `saveDC` and `attackBonus` fields on scroll-shaped consumables, the values move into structured data and the planner consumes them directly.
  - *Concentration handling* (Greater Invisibility, plus any concentration spell wired through scrolls): the consumer becomes the spell's concentration anchor for the scroll-cast spell's duration. This composes cleanly with the existing concentration-tracker once `ConsumeItem` + `ItemSpellGrant` land; no new primitive needed beyond what scrolls share with potion-of-speed-style "Haste without concentration" (here, concentration is preserved rather than suppressed).
  - *Risk-clause spells* (Wish, and arguably Disintegrate for the dust-on-kill clause): the spell itself carries its post-cast complications (Wish's 33% stress arm, the never-cast-Wish-again risk on certain Wish variants). Once Wish wires, the scroll inherits the entire complication chain because it routes through the existing spell wiring rather than reproducing it.
- **Items-batch-1.14 Ioun Stones** (introduces the Ioun Stone family with one passive stone per ability score plus Awareness and Protection). All eight ship as `itemKind: 'magic'` with `effects: []`, all very rare or rare, all attunement-required. The Ioun Stone orbiting mechanic (the stone hovers around the wearer's head and doesn't occupy a body slot) is currently model-implicit: the engine treats Ioun Stones as attuned items with no equipped slot, which already works for the magic-item shape since the slot system is consumer-side metadata. RAW only enforces the orbiting visual narratively; defer that to consumer rendering. Per-stone shapes once magic-item passive effects project to the wearer's stack:
  - *Ability-score Ioun Stones* (Strength, Agility, Fortitude, Intellect, Insight, Leadership): each increases the named ability score by 2, to a maximum of 20. Mechanically distinct from batch 1.2's Belt of Hill Giant Strength (`OverrideAbilityScore { value: 21 }` which sets the score) and batch 1.9's Hammer of Thunderbolts (multi-item attunement setting STR to 27). The Ioun Stones want an `IncreaseAbilityScore { ability: AbilityScore, amount: 2, cap: 20 }` primitive that stacks additively up to a cap, distinct from the set / override semantic. Two different primitives serve the family: `OverrideAbilityScore` for set-to-N items (belt, headband, gauntlets), `IncreaseAbilityScore` for additive-with-cap items (Ioun Stones, Tome of Clear Thought / Quickness / Leadership family that ships in a future batch under the same primitive at `amount: 2, cap: 20` then permanently bumps the user's base score on consumption).
  - *Ioun Stone (Awareness)*: surprise-immunity. RAW: "You can't be surprised while you're conscious." Composes with a `GrantConditionImmunity` shape pointed at the engine's surprise mechanic; in 2024 surprise is a disadvantage on initiative rather than a tracked condition, so the cleanest wiring is a `SuppressInitiativeDisadvantage { source: 'surprise' }` or equivalent predicate that's distinct from the conditional-disadvantage primitive Cloak of Elvenkind / Eyes of the Eagle want (those are checks against the wearer; this is the wearer's own initiative roll).
  - *Ioun Stone (Protection)*: ✓ wired in slice 139 as `AddModifier { target: 'ac', value: 1 }`. No deferred sub-arms.
  - *Deferred Ioun Stones*: the charges-style variants (Absorption stores 20 spell levels, Greater Absorption stores 50, Reserve stores 3 levels of spells) ship in a future charges-bearing batch under a "spell-level-pool" charges shape that's distinct from the integer-charges shape Wand of Magic Missiles uses. The legendary variants (Mastery for +1 proficiency bonus, Regeneration for 15 HP per hour, Greater Absorption) ship in a future batch under primitives the engine doesn't currently model: proficiency-bonus override and out-of-combat regeneration with hourly tick semantics.
- **Items-batch-1.15 rings cohort** (extends the existing Ring of Protection / Swimming / Jumping set with eight more entries spanning uncommon through legendary). All eight ship as `itemKind: 'magic'` with `effects: []` and no charges (the charges-bearing rings — Evasion, Shooting Stars, Spell Storing, Spell Turning, Telekinesis, the Ram, Three Wishes, X-Ray Vision, Animal Influence — defer to a future charges-bearing batch under the existing `UseItem` planner blocker from batch 1.7). Per-ring wiring once magic-item passive effects project to the wearer's stack:
  - *Ring of Feather Falling*: trigger-on-fall mechanic. RAW: "When you fall while wearing this ring, you descend 60 feet per round and take no damage from falling." Needs a fall-event hook the engine doesn't currently expose (falling is consumer-narrative today; the engine has no falling planner or fall-damage derivation) plus an auto-cast of Feather Fall's mechanical effect (no concentration, single-target self). Once a falling primitive lands, the ring composes with Feather Fall's existing spell wiring on the trigger.
  - *Ring of Mind Shielding*: composite detection-immunity. RAW: "You are immune to magic that allows other creatures to read your thoughts, determine whether you are lying, know your alignment, or know your creature type. Creatures can telepathically communicate with you only if you allow it. You can use an action to cause the ring to become invisible." Needs a "telepathic detection" predicate plus Detect Thoughts / Zone of Truth / Detect Alignment-style spell immunity (a spell-list-scoped immunity that's distinct from a single-spell immunity like Brooch of Shielding's magic-missile immunity). The "can become invisible" cosmetic arm is narrative.
  - *Ring of Warmth*: ✓ cold-resistance arm wired in slice 139 as `GrantResistance { damageType: 'cold' }`. The temperature-tolerance-to-zero-F arm stays consumer / DM-discretion narrative (the engine doesn't track ambient temperature).
  - *Ring of Water Walking*: walk-on-liquid-surface mechanic. RAW: "While wearing this ring, you can stand on and move across any liquid surface as if it were solid ground." The engine doesn't currently model walking-on-water as a movement primitive (it's a special movement mode distinct from walk / fly / climb / swim). Needs either a `ModifySpeed { mode: 'water-walk', op: 'add' }` extension to the existing `ModifySpeed` primitive that already handles climb / swim / fly, or a composition with the existing Water Walking spell's continuous effect.
  - *Ring of Free Action*: ✓ paralyzed + restrained immunity arms wired in slice 139 as two `GrantConditionImmunity` entries. The difficult-terrain-movement-cost-suppression arm (a per-bearer predicate distinct from slice 90's terrain-layer doubling) and the magical-speed-reduction-immunity arm (needs a magical-vs-nonmagical distinction on speed reductions the engine doesn't currently model) stay deferred.
  - *Ring of Resistance*: variant-typed resistance. RAW: "You have resistance to one damage type while wearing this ring. The damage type is determined by the ring's gem (sapphire = lightning, jade = poison, pearl = acid, ruby = fire, topaz = cold, etc.)." Same item-instance variant pattern as batch 1.10's Dragon Scale Mail (consumer picks damage type at attunement / pack-load time); composes with `GrantResistance { damageType }` once projection lands. The variant arm is shared with Dragon Scale Mail's deferral.
  - *Ring of Regeneration*: out-of-combat HP regeneration with a 10-minute tick. RAW: "While wearing this ring, you regain 1d6 hit points every 10 minutes, provided that you have at least 1 hit point. If you lose a body part, the ring causes the missing part to regrow and return to full functionality after 1d6+1 days." Same shape as the Ioun Stone Regeneration variant deferred in batch 1.14, but at a 10-minute tick rather than hourly. Needs a continuous-HP-regen primitive scoped to a time interval the engine doesn't currently model (rest mechanics handle long-rest / short-rest healing, but not an in-game 10-minute timer for continuous regen). The limb-regrowth arm is consumer-narrative.
  - *Ring of Invisibility*: toggleable Invisible condition. RAW: "While wearing this ring, you can turn invisible as an action. Anything you are wearing or carrying is invisible with you. You remain invisible until the ring is removed, until you attack or cast a spell, or until you use a bonus action to become visible again." Same Invisible-condition primitive as Potion of Invisibility (already in-pack via the wired Invisibility spell), but toggleable rather than 1-hour timed. Needs a `ToggleItemEffect` planner (the same primitive Driftglobe's continuous-light arm deferred in batch 1.7) plus the "ends on attack or spell" trigger that the Invisibility spell already carries. Once both `ToggleItemEffect` and the spell wiring compose, the ring wires cleanly.
- **Items-batch-1.16 Giant Strength tier extension** (completes the canonical Belt + Potion family that batches 1.2 and 1.12 began with Hill Giant Strength). Five belts (`itemKind: 'magic'`, attunement-required, `effects: []`) and five potions (`itemKind: 'consumable'`, `onConsume: []`) covering the Stone (STR 23), Frost (STR 23), Fire (STR 25), Cloud (STR 27), and Storm (STR 29) tiers. All ten ship as pure stub; snapshot-clean. Mechanically the entire family wires through a single primitive — the `OverrideAbilityScore { ability: 'STR', value: N }` shape that batch 1.2's Belt of Hill Giant Strength named — at six different `value` targets (21 / 23 / 23 / 25 / 27 / 29). The Frost and Stone tiers ship at the same numeric target (STR 23); they're distinct items per RAW because their visual / flavor differs and their crafting components differ, but their mechanical wiring is identical. Belt rarities follow the RAW progression (Hill rare, Stone / Frost / Fire very rare, Cloud / Storm legendary); potion rarities are one tier lower across the board per RAW (Hill uncommon, Stone / Frost / Fire rare, Cloud very rare, Storm legendary), reflecting the 1-hour duration limit on the potion variant. The cross-cutting projection blocker is unchanged from batch 1.2; the consume-side `ConsumeItem` blocker is unchanged from batch 1.3. No new primitives this batch.
- **Items-batch-1.17 robes and outer garments** (the canonical 5e/2024 DMG robe catalog is short — only four robes total, two of which are charges-bearing and deferred — so this batch extends to thematically-adjacent outer-garment slot items: two robes, two cloaks, bracers, two pairs of boots, plus a cosmetic-common armor entry). Per-item shapes once magic-item passive effects project to the wearer's stack:
  - *Robe of the Archmagi*: composite caster-bonus passive. +2 AC, +2 spell save DC, +2 spell attack bonus, advantage on saving throws against spells. The +2 AC arm composes with `AddModifier { target: 'ac', value: 2 }` (the shape Ring of Protection and Cloak of Protection use). The save / attack +2 arms compose with the same `AddModifier` shape targeted at the bearer's spell-save-DC and spell-attack derivations once projection lands. The advantage-on-saves-vs-spells arm composes with `SetAdvantage { on: 'save', savedAgainst: 'spell' }` (the predicate Spellguard Shield wanted in batch 1.10). The class-locked attunement is data-only today (same shape as Holy Avenger from batch 1.9).
  - *Robe of Eyes*: composite passive-sense bundle. 360-degree vision (no flanking-from-behind once flanking is modeled), darkvision 120 ft, see invisible, see ethereal, advantage on Perception checks involving sight, can't be surprised, cannot be blinded by sighted-source effects except by sunlight. Composes with `GrantSense` (slice 127) at multiple sense entries (`darkvision`, `seeInvisible`, `seeEthereal`), `SuppressInitiativeDisadvantage { source: 'surprise' }` from Ioun Stone (Awareness) in batch 1.14, and conditional perception advantage from Sentinel Shield in batch 1.10. The sunlight-blinded sub-arm needs an action-tagged-as-looking trigger that fires the Blinded condition when the wearer takes a Search-like action while looking at a sunlight source.
  - *Cloak of Displacement*: bearer-protective disadvantage on incoming attacks. Same third-party-perspective conditional-disadvantage shape as Cloak of Elvenkind (batch 1.1, perception-scoped) and Spellguard Shield (batch 1.10, spell-attack-scoped), but here scoped to all incoming weapon attacks. Plus the "ceases to function until your next turn after you take damage" arm needs a damage-event-triggered suspension on the projected effect (a new "effect goes dormant for N rounds on trigger event T" primitive that generalizes to several other RAW items in the long tail).
  - *Cloak of Billowing*: cosmetic dramatic billow. Pure narrative; no engine wiring needed.
  - *Bracers of Defense*: conditional +2 AC. RAW: "+2 bonus to AC if you are wearing no armor and using no shield." Composes with the same `AddModifier { target: 'ac', value: 2 }` shape Ring of Protection uses, gated by a predicate on the bearer's equipment state (`bearer.armorEquipped == null && bearer.shieldEquipped == null`). The predicate is consumer-side metadata until the engine's equipped-item shape gains explicit slot accessors.
  - *Boots of Levitation*: at-will Levitate spell. Same `ItemSpellGrant { spellId: 'levitate', atWill: true }` primitive Hat of Disguise wanted in batch 1.2.
  - *Boots of Speed*: toggleable speed-doubling with per-day time budget plus opportunity-attack disadvantage. Composes with `ModifySpeed { mode: 'walk', op: 'multiply', factor: 2 }` (the multiply op was named for `Boots of Striding and Springing` batch-1.2 deferral); the OAs-against-you disadvantage is the same third-party-perspective shape Cloak of Displacement wants but scoped to opportunity attacks specifically; the per-day 10-minute time budget is the same `timeBudget` shape Winged Boots wanted in batch 1.4, plus a `ToggleItemEffect` planner the Driftglobe / Ring of Invisibility deferred.
  - *Smoldering Armor*: cosmetic smoke wisps when the wearer takes damage. Pure narrative; no engine wiring needed.

The batch pulls the natural slot-adjacent cohort (neck-slot amulets / periapts / talismans, face / head / hands / feet small accessories) so the cloak shipment doesn't ship as a singleton.   - *Amulet of Proof Against Detection and Location*: composite divination-immunity. RAW: "You are hidden from divination magic. You can't be targeted by such magic or perceived through magical scrying sensors." Same spell-list-scoped immunity predicate Ring of Mind Shielding wanted in batch 1.15, but scoped to the divination school rather than telepathic spells specifically (the engine has spells tagged with school in its catalog, so the predicate `event.spell.school === 'divination'` is the natural extension once spell-school is exposed as a fact).

The at-will spell arm composes with `ItemSpellGrant { spellId: 'comprehend-languages', atWill: true }` (the Hat of Disguise / Boots of Levitation primitive). The conditional advantage arm composes with `SetAdvantage { on: 'check', skill: 'insight' }` gated by a spoken-word predicate.
  - *Gloves of Thievery*: +5 to specific Dexterity checks. RAW: "+5 bonus to Dexterity (Sleight of Hand) checks and Dexterity checks made to pick locks." Composes with `AddModifier { target: 'check', value: 5 }` gated by a per-check-kind predicate (Sleight of Hand and lockpicking specifically, not all DEX checks). The lockpicking arm is currently consumer-side (the engine doesn't separate lockpicking from other DEX checks); the Sleight of Hand arm wires straightforwardly via the skill predicate.
  - *Boots of the Winterlands*: ✓ cold-resistance arm wired in slice 139 as `GrantResistance { damageType: 'cold' }`. The ice-and-snow difficult-terrain-ignore arm (needs `IgnoreDifficultTerrain { source: 'ice-or-snow' }` per-bearer predicate, distinct from slice 90's terrain-layer doubling) and the temperature-tolerance arm (consumer-narrative as in Ring of Warmth) stay deferred.
- **Items-batch-1.19 charges-bearing rings** (the followup to the batch-1.15 pure-stub rings cohort, completing the rings catalog with the charges-bearing variants). All eight ship as `itemKind: 'magic'` with the canonical RAW charges shape (5 / never for Spell Storing's spell-level pool, 3 / never for Three Wishes as a single-use-pool ring matching Gem of Brightness's exhaust-then-mundane shape, 1 / longRest for X-Ray Vision, 3 + 1d3 / dawn for Ram / Evasion / Animal Influence / Spell Turning, 6 / dawn for Shooting Stars). Snapshot refresh required (the diff is exactly eight new IDs added to `withChargesIds`); the per-ring action wiring routes through the same `UseItem` planner already deferred for batches 1.7 and 1.11. Per-ring shapes once `UseItem` lands:
  - *Ring of Spell Storing*: stores up to 5 levels of spell into the ring, casts stored spells out as actions. Schema models the level count as charges (max: 5, recharge: never), but the actual mechanic is a spell-level pool: when a spellcaster casts a spell into the ring, the ring stores it and consumes the spell's level from its remaining capacity; when the wearer casts out, the stored spell fires using the ring's stored DC and the level it was stored at. The "refills via inbound cast" arm is a novel shape — distinct from both `RegainSpellSlot` (Pearl of Power) and standard charges. Needs a `CastSpellIntoItem` planner that consumes the caster's spell slot, validates the level fits in the remaining pool, and stores the spell-id + cast-level + DC tuple inside the item-instance.
  - *Ring of Three Wishes*: 3 charges of Wish on a single-use pool that never refills. RAW: "becomes a nonmagical band when the last charge is used." Same exhaust-then-mundane shape as Gem of Brightness from batch 1.7; needs the same item-disenchant-on-empty primitive (`OnExhaust { result: 'remove-magic' }`). Per-charge effect is `ItemSpellGrant { spellId: 'wish' }` once that primitive lands.
  - *Ring of X-Ray Vision*: 1 use per long rest of see-through-walls. The vision-grant arm composes with `GrantSense { sense: 'x-ray', range: 30 }` (slice 127's shape extended to a new sense kind) on a 1-minute duration condition. The post-effect "DC 15 CON save or 1 level of exhaustion" arm composes with the post-effect chained-follow-up-condition primitive Potion of Speed's batch-1.12 deferral named.
  - *Ring of the Ram*: variable-charge attack with three usage tiers. Spend 1-3 charges as an action for a ranged push attack (1 charge = 1d10 force + 5 ft push, 2 charges = 2d10 + 10 ft, 3 charges = 3d10 + 15 ft). Needs the variable-charges-per-cast extension Wand of Fireballs deferred in batch 1.11 plus a directional-push primitive distinct from the `Shove` action (this is a ranged push, not a melee one).
  - *Ring of Evasion*: 1-charge reaction to succeed on a failed DEX save. Composes with `RewriteSaveResult { from: 'fail', to: 'success' }` on the wearer's reaction-window, the same shape as Cloak of Protection's "+1 to all saves" but operating on the outcome rather than the modifier (and gated to DEX saves specifically per RAW). New primitive.
  - *Ring of Animal Influence*: variable-cost spell-grant. 1 charge for Animal Friendship, 1 for Speak with Animals, 2 for Fear (limited to beasts only). Composes with `ItemSpellGrant` at per-spell save-DC override (DC 13 per RAW for the ring), plus the variable-charge cost extension Wand of Fireballs / Ring of the Ram want.
  - *Ring of Shooting Stars*: multi-effect charge pool with dusk-only activation. Three sub-effects each with their own charge cost (Faerie Fire 1, Ball Lightning 1-4, Shooting Stars 2). Needs the multi-action item shape Gem of Brightness deferred in batch 1.7 plus a time-of-day predicate (dusk-only activation) that's distinct from the existing day / night fact (`when: 'dusk-or-darker'`).
  - *Ring of Spell Turning*: reaction to redirect or absorb a targeted spell. Spend 1-3 charges to redirect a spell back at the caster (DC 17 caster-save or be hit by their own spell, per 2014 RAW). Composes with the spell-reflection shape (a new primitive — no existing item or feature in the engine reflects spells back at the caster) plus the variable-charge cost extension.
- **Items-batch-1.20 charges-bearing wand expansion** (extends the batch-1.11 wand cohort with eight more entries, primarily save-or-suffer spell-grant wands). All eight share the canonical 7-charges / 1d6+1-at-dawn shape (Fear / Lightning Bolts / Paralysis / Polymorph / Binding / Pyrotechnics) or the 3-charges / 1d3-at-dawn shape (Enemy Detection / Secrets), matching the existing Wand of Magic Missiles and Wand of Fireballs precedents. Most route through the same `ItemSpellGrant` primitive Staff of Charming and the batch-1.11 wands need. Per-wand shapes once `UseItem` lands:
  - *Wand of Fear*, *Wand of Lightning Bolts*, *Wand of Paralysis*, *Wand of Polymorph*, *Wand of Binding*: variants of the same shape Wand of Fireballs / Wand of Web wanted in batch 1.11 — `ItemSpellGrant { spellId, saveDC: 15 }` (or DC 17 for Polymorph per RAW) with charge consumption gated by the cast. Polymorph specifically also wants the target-creature-form variant that the Polymorph spell wiring carries; the wand inherits that complication chain via the spell wiring. No new primitives beyond the batch-1.11 set; this batch deepens the wand catalog rather than introducing novel shapes.
  - *Wand of Enemy Detection*: 1-charge action grants a 1-minute "sense of enemies within 60 ft" effect. Composes with `GrantSense { sense: 'enemy-detection', range: 60 }` (slice-127 shape extended to a new sense kind; same pattern as Ring of X-Ray Vision's batch-1.19 deferral). Plus a "creatures hostile to the wielder" predicate distinct from the alignment-detect shape Amulet of Proof Against Detection wants — this one is intent-based ("hostile to you specifically"), not alignment-based.
  - *Wand of Secrets*: 1-charge action reveals a hidden door or trap within 30 ft. Composes with a "detect-hidden-object" primitive scoped to the wielder's narrative-discoverable secrets within a range. The engine doesn't currently track hidden-door / hidden-trap state at the entity layer (these are typically narrated by the DM during exploration); pure consumer-side narrative until a discoverable-object schema lands.
  - *Wand of Pyrotechnics*: 1-charge action creates a fireworks display or smoke cloud. Two sub-effects branching on the wielder's choice at activation (pyrotechnics vs smoke cloud). Pyrotechnics: 1d4 fire damage to creatures within 5 ft and blinded condition (DC 13 CON save) for the round. Smoke cloud: 20-ft sphere of obscured terrain for 1 minute. Composes with `ItemSpellGrant` routed to a sub-spell catalog (a wand-internal effect list rather than a published spell), plus a target-selection-at-activation arm distinct from the bearer-vs-surface branch Oil of Slipperiness wanted in batch 1.12 (this one selects between two distinct effect sets).
