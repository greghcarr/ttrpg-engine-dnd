# Monster gaps: MM seed + content-monsters batches 1.1-1.21 (alpha.5 seed + earliest post-alpha.5 batches)

Per-batch catalog of monsters added in the MM seed (alpha.5) and batches 1.1 through 1.21. Extracted from [starter-pack-gaps.md](starter-pack-gaps.md) in slice 249.

This window covers: MM beasts seed (alpha.5 era — 6 entries), 1.1 NPC roster (10), 1.2 NPC roster (5), 1.3 Bestiary (8), 1.4 Caster NPCs (now 3 after slice 141), 1.5 Bestiary tier-up (6), 1.6 Low-CR Undead variety (5), 1.7 Low-CR Fiend introduction (now 4 after slice 141), 1.8 Giant cohort (6), 1.9 Elemental cohort (5), 1.10 Monstrosity opener (6), 1.11 Ooze cohort (4), 1.12 Construct ladder (6), 1.13 Aberration opener (now 3 after slice 141), 1.14 Fey cohort (now 4 after slice 141), 1.15 Celestial cohort (6), 1.16 Plant cohort (4), 1.17 Young Chromatic Dragon tier-up (4), 1.18 Adult Chromatic Dragon tier-up (5), 1.19 Devil ladder tier-up (now 4 after slice 141), 1.20 Beast megafauna tier-up (6), 1.21 Humanoid combat tier-up (2).

For batches 4.x, see [gaps-monsters-batches-4.1-4.7.md](gaps-monsters-batches-4.1-4.7.md) and [gaps-monsters-batches-4.8-4.14.md](gaps-monsters-batches-4.8-4.14.md). For batches 5.x, see [gaps-monsters-batches-5.9-5.11.md](gaps-monsters-batches-5.9-5.11.md), [gaps-monsters-batches-5.6-5.8.md](gaps-monsters-batches-5.6-5.8.md), and [gaps-monsters-batches-5.1-5.5.md](gaps-monsters-batches-5.1-5.5.md). For the deferred-mechanics catalog, see [gaps-monsters-deferred-mechanics.md](gaps-monsters-deferred-mechanics.md).

**MM beasts (6):** Goblin Warrior (CR 1/4; renamed from Goblin in slice 142), Wolf (CR 1/4), Skeleton (CR 1/4), Ogre (CR 2), Young Red Dragon (CR 10), and one entry (Orc) since dropped in slice 141 (Orc removed from SRD 5.2.1 monster catalog).

**NPC roster, content-monsters-batch-1.1 (10):** Bandit (CR 1/8), Bandit Captain (CR 2), Cultist (CR 1/8), Cult Fanatic (CR 2; renamed to Cultist Fanatic in slice 142), Acolyte (CR 1/4; renamed to Priest Acolyte in slice 142), Commoner (CR 0), Guard (CR 1/8), Noble (CR 1/8), Scout (CR 1/2), Spy (CR 1).

**NPC roster, content-monsters-batch-1.2 (5):** Tribal Warrior (CR 1/8; renamed to Warrior Infantry in slice 142), Thug (CR 1/2; renamed to Tough in slice 142), Berserker (CR 2), Veteran (CR 3; renamed to Warrior Veteran in slice 142), Knight (CR 3). Closes the humanoid roster up through CR 3.

**Bestiary, content-monsters-batch-1.3 (8):** Zombie (CR 1/4), Ghoul (CR 1), Giant Rat (CR 1/8), Giant Spider (CR 1), Giant Wolf Spider (CR 1/4), Brown Bear (CR 1), Dire Wolf (CR 1), Stirge (CR 1/8). First broad fill outside humanoids: 6 Beasts + 2 Undead, covering the most-encountered low-CR creature types.

**Caster NPCs, content-monsters-batch-1.4 (originally 4, now 3 after slice 141):** Priest (CR 2), Druid (CR 2), Mage (CR 6). Apprentice Wizard dropped in slice 141 (not in SRD 5.2.1). Closes the spell-using humanoid NPC gap. All three hit the existing monster-spellcasting deferral (no new primitive needed to wire if monster spellcasting ever lands).

**Bestiary tier-up, content-monsters-batch-1.5 (6):** Mastiff (CR 1/8), Boar (CR 1/4), Constrictor Snake (CR 1/4), Giant Frog (CR 1/4), Giant Centipede (CR 1/4), Black Bear (CR 1/2). Fills the low-CR Beast pile out to encounter-table density for wilderness / dungeon ambient threats.

**Low-CR Undead variety, content-monsters-batch-1.6 (5):** Shadow (CR 1/2), Specter (CR 1), Wight (CR 3), Ghost (CR 4), Wraith (CR 5). Fills the Undead pile out to a usable haunted-location roster. First batch to populate `traits` on monster statblocks: all five carry `GrantResistance` entries with `qualifier: 'nonmagical'` for B/P/S (slice 112 effect shape). This data is currently inert at runtime (see the cross-cutting structural-gap note below), but ships RAW-accurate so it wires for free when the engine consumes monster data.

**Low-CR Fiend introduction, content-monsters-batch-1.7 (originally 5, now 4 after slice 141):** Lemure (CR 0), Dretch (CR 1/4), Imp (CR 1), Quasit (CR 1). Manes dropped in slice 141 (not in SRD 5.2.1). Opens the Fiend creature type with the canonical low-tier demons and devils, including the familiar pair (Imp / Quasit). Three of the four (Imp, Lemure, Quasit) carry the slice-112 `GrantResistance` B/P/S nonmagical traits; Imp and Lemure's RAW "not made with silvered weapons" further qualifier is deferred (same gap as Wight in batch 1.6). Subtype field exercised for the first time (`subtype: "devil"`, `"demon"`, `"devil, shapechanger"`, `"demon, shapechanger"`).

**Giant cohort, content-monsters-batch-1.8 (6):** Hill Giant (CR 5), Stone Giant (CR 7), Frost Giant (CR 8), Fire Giant (CR 9), Cloud Giant (CR 9), Storm Giant (CR 13). Opens the Giant creature type with the canonical six elemental-affinity giants, covering CR 5-13 as a single coherent set. Exercises damage immunities (Frost: cold; Fire: fire; Storm: lightning + thunder), damage resistances (Storm: cold), and the full save / skill / sense kit on every entry. First time the pack carries a CR 13 statblock. All six ship `traits: []` pending Multiattack / monster-action vocabulary; the elemental-affinity damage-modifier data is RAW-accurate and will wire for free when the engine consumes `monster.damageImmunities` / `damageResistances` (see cross-cutting structural-gap note below).

**Elemental cohort, content-monsters-batch-1.9 (5):** Air Elemental (CR 5), Earth Elemental (CR 5), Fire Elemental (CR 5), Water Elemental (CR 5), Invisible Stalker (CR 6). Opens the Elemental creature type with all four base elementals plus the Invisible Stalker as the canonical CR 6 air-elemental variant. Every entry carries the slice-112 `GrantResistance` B/P/S nonmagical traits, plus elemental-affinity damage modifiers: Air resists lightning / thunder; Earth takes thunder vulnerability (first new vulnerability entry beyond the seed Skeleton's bludgeoning); Fire is immune to fire; Water resists acid. Every entry is immune to poison and to a near-complete suite of physical-state conditions (exhaustion, grappled, paralyzed, petrified, poisoned, prone, restrained, unconscious). First time `damageVulnerabilities` is populated post-seed and first time `tremorsense` appears (Earth Elemental 60 ft).

**Monstrosity opener, content-monsters-batch-1.10 (6):** Ankheg (CR 2), Bulette (CR 5), Griffon (CR 2), Manticore (CR 3), Minotaur (CR 3), Owlbear (CR 3). Opens the Monstrosity creature type with six adventure-staple iconic creatures in the CR 2-5 sweet spot (the underrepresented wilderness / dungeon middle band of the pack). All six ship as bare statblocks (`traits: []`) pending Multiattack and the on-hit save / damage rider primitives. Three of six carry the burrow / fly / tremorsense speed and sense kit (Ankheg, Bulette, Griffon). Languages: only Manticore (Common) and Minotaur (Abyssal) speak.

**Ooze cohort, content-monsters-batch-1.11 (4):** Black Pudding (CR 4), Gelatinous Cube (CR 2), Gray Ooze (CR 1/2), Ochre Jelly (CR 2). Closes the Ooze creature type with the canonical four. Every entry shares a tight mechanical pattern: blindsight 60 (blind beyond), the full ooze condition-immunity profile (blinded, charmed, deafened, exhaustion, frightened, prone), Amorphous movement (deferred under the existing narrative-movement note), and a Pseudopod attack with acid damage. Damage profiles diverge by type: Black Pudding (immune acid / cold / lightning / slashing), Gelatinous Cube (no resistances or immunities), Gray Ooze (resist acid / cold / fire), Ochre Jelly (resist acid; immune lightning / slashing). First time the pack carries `damageImmunities: ["slashing"]` (Black Pudding, Ochre Jelly) and `damageImmunities: ["lightning"]`. All four ship `traits: []`.

**Construct ladder, content-monsters-batch-1.12 (6):** Animated Armor (CR 1), Flying Sword (CR 1/4; renamed to Animated Flying Sword in slice 142), Rug of Smothering (CR 2; renamed to Animated Rug of Smothering in slice 142), Shield Guardian (CR 7), Stone Golem (CR 10), Iron Golem (CR 16). Opens the Construct creature type across a wide CR 1/4-16 band; first time the pack carries a CR 16 statblock. The three low-tier constructs (Animated Armor, Animated Flying Sword, Animated Rug of Smothering) share the canonical "animated object" condition-immunity suite (blinded, charmed, deafened, exhaustion, frightened, paralyzed, petrified, poisoned) plus poison and psychic damage immunity and blindsight 60 (blind beyond). The two Golems carry slice-112 `GrantResistance` B/P/S nonmagical traits (RAW: "not adamantine" further qualifier deferred, same gap as Wight's silvered qualifier in batch 1.6) plus poison / psychic immunity and an expanded condition-immunity profile (no blinded / deafened, gains petrified). Iron Golem also has fire immunity, healing from fire damage deferred separately. The Animated Rug of Smothering's slashing vulnerability is the second post-seed `damageVulnerabilities` entry (joining Earth Elemental's thunder).

**Aberration opener, content-monsters-batch-1.13 (originally 5, now 3 after slice 141):** Aboleth (CR 10), Gibbering Mouther (CR 2), Otyugh (CR 5). Beholder and Mind Flayer dropped in slice 141 (WotC trademarks, never in any SRD). Opened the Aberration creature type. Aboleth carries telepathy as a `languages` array entry pending a proper directional-sense / language-vs-sense schema split; Otyugh carries its single fictional language `otyugh` with "limited telepathy" deferred separately. Gibbering Mouther is immune to the prone condition (the amorphous biology). All three ship `traits: []`. Original batch-1.13 deferral-discovery footprint, now narrower since Beholder + Mind Flayer dropped: still opens Legendary Actions and Legendary Resistance (now via Aboleth alone in this batch; Adult Chromatics in batch 1.18 broadened the user list), Enslave with recurring save (Aboleth, same shape as Quasit Scare), and a half-dozen narrative riders. Antimagic Cone, Eye Rays, Mind Blast, Tentacle stun on grapple, and Extract Brain were Beholder/Mind-Flayer-only deferrals; they're dropped from the queue now that their canonical users are gone.

**Fey cohort, content-monsters-batch-1.14 (originally 5, now 4 after slice 141):** Blink Dog (CR 1/4), Dryad (CR 1), Satyr (CR 1/2), Sprite (CR 1/4). Pixie dropped in slice 141 (not in SRD 5.2.1). Opens the Fey creature type with the canonical low-CR roster. Two of four (Dryad, Satyr) carry Magic Resistance, folded into the existing entry. Dryad carries a per-spell envelope innate spellcasting list (Druid casting flavor); Couatl in batch 1.15 is now the densest single-monster spell list in the pack at 13 entries. All four ship `traits: []`.

**Celestial cohort, content-monsters-batch-1.15 (6):** Pegasus (CR 2), Couatl (CR 4), Unicorn (CR 5), Deva (CR 10), Planetar (CR 16), Solar (CR 21). Opens the Celestial creature type across the widest CR band yet — Solar at CR 21 is the highest-CR statblock in the pack (previously Iron Golem at 16). The four angels (Couatl, Deva, Planetar, Solar) carry slice-112 `GrantResistance` B/P/S nonmagical traits plus radiant resistance and condition-immunity profiles canonical to Celestials (charmed / exhaustion / frightened, plus poison-immunity on Solar). Couatl ships truesight 120 (first time the senses field exercises this key) and `all languages` + telepathy 120 ft (the angels share this language profile). Solar carries the highest XP in the pack (33000) and the highest proficiency bonus (+7). Pegasus and Unicorn ship `traits: []`; the four angels populate the standard B/P/S nonmagical resistance traits.

**Plant cohort, content-monsters-batch-1.16 (4):** Awakened Shrub (CR 0), Awakened Tree (CR 2), Shambling Mound (CR 5), Treant (CR 9). Closes the Plant creature type with the canonical SRD four, completing every MM creature type that has SRD-licensed entries (the pack now covers every creature type at least once). Three of four (Awakened Shrub, Awakened Tree, Treant) ship `damageVulnerabilities: ["fire"]` — the largest single-batch population of the vulnerability field and the third-through-fifth post-seed entries (joining Skeleton's bludgeoning, Earth Elemental's thunder, Animated Rug of Smothering's slashing). Three of four also carry plain (unqualified) `damageResistances` for bludgeoning / piercing rather than the slice-112 `GrantResistance.qualifier` form, since RAW Plant resistance is unconditional, not source-qualified. Shambling Mound exercises lightning immunity (with Lightning Absorption healing deferred separately) plus cold / fire resistance and blindsight 60. All four ship `traits: []`.

**Young Chromatic Dragon tier-up, content-monsters-batch-1.17 (4):** Young White Dragon (CR 6), Young Black Dragon (CR 7), Young Green Dragon (CR 8), Young Blue Dragon (CR 9). First tier-up-within-existing-type batch (every prior content batch opened a new type or expanded the humanoid roster). Closes the Young Chromatic Dragon set started by the seed's Young Red Dragon at CR 10, mirroring its statblock shape: Large Dragon, blindsight 30 + darkvision 120, expertise on Perception, multi-mode speed (each color has its own walk / burrow / fly / swim mix appropriate to its environment), Common + Draconic. Each ships its canonical elemental immunity (cold / acid / poison / lightning). Young Green also adds the poisoned condition immunity (the only Young Chromatic with one). All four ship `traits: []`. The pack now reaches one hundred statblocks (round-number milestone).

**Adult Chromatic Dragon tier-up, content-monsters-batch-1.18 (5):** Adult White Dragon (CR 13), Adult Black Dragon (CR 14), Adult Green Dragon (CR 15), Adult Blue Dragon (CR 16), Adult Red Dragon (CR 17). Closes the entire chromatic Adult Dragon ladder in a single batch. Each is Huge size, blindsight 60 + darkvision 120, expertise on Perception, canonical elemental immunity, and Common + Draconic. Adult Green additionally gains poisoned condition immunity. The cohort introduces the first batch where every entry uses Legendary Actions and Legendary Resistance (3/day each) and exercises Frightful Presence (a new mechanic-level deferral introduced this batch). Adult Red's proficiency bonus +6 is the second-highest in the pack (after Solar's +7). The five Adult Chromatics share an exact statblock template with Adult White / Black / Green / Blue / Red scaling parameterized only by CR, ability scores, breath shape / DC / dice, bite-rider die, and Frightful Presence DC — making them the highest-density per-data tier-up in the pack so far.

**Devil ladder tier-up, content-monsters-batch-1.19 (originally 5, now 4 after slice 141):** Bearded Devil (CR 3), Barbed Devil (CR 5), Chain Devil (CR 8), Bone Devil (CR 9). Spined Devil dropped in slice 141 (not in SRD 5.2.1). Closes the mid-tier Devil hierarchy started by Imp (CR 1) and Lemure (CR 0) in batch 1.7. All four share the canonical Devil damage profile (cold resistance, fire / poison immunity, B/P/S nonmagical resistance via slice-112 `GrantResistance` traits with the "not silvered" further qualifier deferred per the established Wight / Imp gap, poisoned condition immunity, darkvision 120, Infernal + telepathy 120 ft, Magic Resistance, Devil's Sight). The Bone Devil is the first Large devil in the pack (joining the existing low-tier Tiny / Small / Medium fiends). Each carries a distinctive signature action that surfaces a new mechanic-level deferral.

**Beast megafauna tier-up, content-monsters-batch-1.20 (6):** Pteranodon (CR 1/4), Plesiosaurus (CR 2), Allosaurus (CR 2), Triceratops (CR 5), Mammoth (CR 6), Tyrannosaurus Rex (CR 8). Closes the high-CR Beast gap with the canonical SRD dinosaurs (Pteranodon, Plesiosaurus, Allosaurus, Triceratops, T-Rex) plus Mammoth as the prehistoric-megafauna companion. Beast cohort prior to this batch capped at Brown Bear / Dire Wolf at CR 1; the pack now ranges through CR 8 in Beasts. Tyrannosaurus Rex is the first Beast with Multiattack. All six ship `traits: []`. Three of six (Allosaurus Pounce, Triceratops Trampling Charge, Mammoth Trampling Charge) extend the existing Charge deferral; T-Rex's bite-auto-grapple with restricted-target-of-bite folds into the existing Ankheg-shaped auto-grapple gap.

**Humanoid combat tier-up, content-monsters-batch-1.21 (2):** Gladiator (CR 5), Assassin (CR 8). Closes the humanoid combat tier-up gap explicitly called out in the gap-notes tail. Both ship `traits: []`. The Gladiator extends the Bandit Captain / Knight / Warrior Veteran / Berserker / Tough humanoid combatant ladder up through CR 5 with three-attack Multiattack, Parry reaction, and shield-bash-with-prone; the Assassin closes the high-CR humanoid rogue-archetype slot with Sneak Attack, Evasion, Assassinate, and poisoned weapon attacks. Both notable for borrowing extensive class-feature shapes (Brave / Parry / Sneak Attack / Evasion / Cunning Action exist as class features; the monster-borrow pathway is the established deferral). One new mechanic-level deferral: Assassinate (composite of first-turn-vs-uninitiated-target advantage + auto-crit-on-surprised target).
