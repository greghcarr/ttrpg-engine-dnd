**Mid-tier Monstrosity sweep, content-monsters-batch-4.14 (6):** Chimera (CR 6), Drider (CR 6), Medusa (CR 6), Hydra (CR 8), Behir (CR 11), Remorhaz (CR 11). Extends the Monstrosity ladder from the batch-4.13 low-tier sweep into the mid-to-upper-mid tier (CR 6 to 11). The Monstrosity roster moves from 17 to 23 entries (Beast at ~18 remains the largest pack roster outside the Dragon ladder, then Monstrosity at 23, then Humanoid at 20). Every entry sourced verbatim from `references/srd-markdown/monsters-A-Z.md` on AC / HP / abilities / saves / damage R/I/V / condition immunities / senses / CR / XP / PB. Type-tagging note: SRD 5.2.1 reclassified several traditionally-Monstrosity creatures (Gorgon → Construct, Troll → Giant, Lamia → Fiend, Roper → Aberration); the batch ships only entries SRD-typed as Monstrosity. Pack monster total moves from 175 to 181. Cohort highlights: Behir's CR 11 with 168 HP + lightning immunity + draconic language + Lightning Breath (90-ft line, DEX DC 16, 12d10) is the first non-Dragon Lightning Breath user in the pack and folds cleanly into the slice-140 breath weapon primitive — but the batch ships `traits: []` on Behir as a coherence choice (the breath weapon is an action rather than a baseline trait; wiring would mirror the dragon-tier breath wiring but is deferred under the dragon-vs-monstrosity breath-weapon-coherence question). Chimera's three-headed Multiattack (Ram + Bite + Claw, with Claw-replaceable-by-Fire-Breath) is the first three-flavor-per-multiattack monster (the existing Adult Chromatic Dragons + Adult Metallics replace-one-attack-with-secondary-breath shape; Chimera is the first multi-flavor variant where each "head" has its own attack type) — exercises the same multiattack-with-options sub-shape as the Metallic Dragons but with three distinct attack flavors rather than weapon-plus-breath-swap. Chimera Bite advantage-on-hit-becomes-4d6+4 introduces the first "if-attack-had-advantage-doubled-damage" rider in the pack — folds into the on-hit-conditional-damage gap with a new advantage-source predicate. Chimera Ram's prone-on-hit-against-Medium-or-smaller folds into the existing on-hit-save-or-condition gap (size-conditional prone). Chimera Fire Breath (Recharge 5-6, 15-ft cone, DEX DC 15, 7d8) is a dragon-style breath on a non-Dragon — same shape as the slice-140 primitive, deferred for now under the dragon-vs-monstrosity-breath coherence question. Drider's Foreleg + Poison Burst attacks (melee + ranged variants per Multiattack), Magic of the Spider Queen (Recharge 5-6 spell-cast Darkness / Faerie Fire / Web at WIS DC 14), Spider Climb (narrative), Sunlight Sensitivity (Disadvantage on ability checks + attack rolls in sunlight — folds into the existing Shadow / Wight / Specter sunlight-sensitivity gap as the eighth user), and Web Walker (narrative). Medusa's iconic Petrifying Gaze (Recharge 5-6, DC 13 CON in 30-ft cone, two-failure escalation Restrained → Petrified) folds into the Petrification family from batch 4.13 (Basilisk / Cockatrice / Medusa); Medusa's self-target-on-reflection mechanic is identical-shape to Basilisk's — both monsters share the gap-side mirror-vulnerability trigger. Medusa's three-attack Multiattack (two Claws + one Snake Hair OR three Poison Rays) introduces the first or-branch Multiattack option in the pack (the closest existing analog is the multiattack-with-secondary-breath swap, but Medusa's is a strict either-melee-or-ranged Multiattack rather than a swap-one-attack model). Medusa carries WIS save proficiency (the first non-Dragon non-Fiend Monstrosity with a save proficiency in the pack). Hydra Multiple Heads + Reactive Heads + regrow-heads-unless-fire-damage form the canonical hydra mechanic — composite of `Multiple Heads` (5-head head-tracker state + die-when-25-damage-or-more-on-a-turn rule + dies-when-all-heads-dead win condition + grow-2-new-heads-per-dead-head-each-turn-unless-fire-damage-this-turn regrow rule), `Reactive Heads` (extra reactions equal to heads-beyond-one for opportunity attacks only), and Multiattack-equals-head-count — three novel composite mechanics that fold into the existing head-count tracker family with a per-head-state primitive that doesn't exist today. Hydra also ships the same 6-condition-immunity profile as Death Dog from batch 4.13 (blinded / charmed / deafened / frightened / stunned / unconscious — the canonical "primitive-body" condition-immunity bundle). Hydra Hold Breath 1-hour trait is narrative. Behir Constrict (DC 18 STR save, fail = 5d8+6 bludgeoning + Grappled DC 16 + Restrained-until-grapple-ends) is the densest single-save Grappled-and-Restrained composite in the pack. Behir Swallow (DC 18 DEX save against a creature already Grappled, on fail target is swallowed; while swallowed, target has Blinded + Restrained conditions, Total Cover against outside attacks, and takes 6d6 acid at start of each Behir turn; if Behir takes 30+ damage on a single turn from swallowed creature, Behir saves DC 14 CON or regurgitates — densest swallow-cycle in the pack, joining the existing Giant Frog Swallow + Remorhaz Swallow gaps). Remorhaz's Heat Aura (5-ft emanation 3d10 fire at end of each turn) folds into the Hezrou Stench / Balor Fire Aura / Pit Fiend Fear Aura aura-trigger family — fourth user of the aura-trigger primitive after batches 4.1 + 4.2. Remorhaz's bite-then-Swallow composite is the same shape as Behir's Bite + Swallow with 5d8+5 acid + 5d8 fire damage and a 30+-damage regurgitate save, plus a "up to two creatures swallowed at a time" cap that introduces a per-statblock swallow-target-cap primitive parameterization. Deferred under existing engine gaps: Multiattack on all six (Chimera 3-flavor, Drider 3-attack-any-combination, Medusa or-branch, Hydra head-count-equals-bites, Behir Bite-plus-Constrict, Remorhaz single-Bite with bonus-action Swallow); Recharge 5-6 area-save breaths on Behir + Chimera (both wireable under the slice-140 primitive but deferred for coherence as noted); poison damage riders on Drider Foreleg + Poison Burst + Medusa Snake Hair + Poison Ray (all fold into the existing poison-damage-rider gap from batch 4.13).

**Low-CR Monstrosity sweep, content-monsters-batch-4.13 (5):** Cockatrice (CR 1/2), Death Dog (CR 1), Mimic (CR 2), Basilisk (CR 3), Phase Spider (CR 3). Opens the low-CR-to-mid Monstrosity tier with five canonical adventure-staple Monstrosities below CR 4. The Monstrosity type roster moves from 12 to 17 entries (joining the existing Stirge / Ankheg / Griffon / Manticore / Owlbear / Bulette + Minotaur of Baphomet + the dinosaur cohort + the Lycanthropes). Every entry sourced verbatim from `references/srd-markdown/monsters-A-Z.md` on AC / HP / abilities / saves / damage R/I/V / condition immunities / senses / CR / XP / PB. Cockatrice + Basilisk + Phase Spider ship `alignment: "unaligned"` (Beast-like instincts); Death Dog is NE (intelligent malice); Mimic is true-neutral (Monstrosity-flavored ambient). Pack monster total moves from 170 to 175. Cohort highlights several mechanic-level deferrals: (1) Petrification riders — Basilisk Petrifying Gaze (DC 12 CON save in 30-ft Cone, recharge 4-6, two-failure escalation: Restrained on first fail then Petrified on second; the basilisk-sees-its-reflection self-target check is a brand-new self-applied save trigger) and Cockatrice Petrifying Bite (DC 11 CON save on hit, two-failure escalation: Restrained on first then Petrified for 24 hours on second). Both fold into the two-failure-escalation save shape introduced by the Brass Sleep + Silver Paralyzing breath weapons in batches 4.5-4.8 and the slice-4.10 lycanthrope curse, parameterized with the Petrified condition. The basilisk's self-targeting on-reflection mechanic is a novel composite (the closest existing analog is the existing Vampire Forbiddance / Sunlight environmental gates, but applied as a self-vulnerability trigger to its own gaze). (2) Death Dog Bite disease (DC 12 CON save, fail = Poisoned + HP-max-no-restore-on-long-rest, repeats per 24 hours; subsequent failures decrease HP max by 1d10) — folds into the Mummy / Mummy Lord cursed-HP-max-decay-over-time family from batch 4.9 with a Poisoned-condition lock and a per-failure stacking decrement rather than a per-24-hour decrement. Death Dog also carries the broadest condition-immunity profile for a CR 1 monster in the pack (blinded / charmed / deafened / frightened / stunned / unconscious — six entries), exercising the absence of poisoned / paralyzed / petrified / exhaustion / prone — a distinctive non-Undead condition-immunity profile. (3) Mimic Adhesive trait (Object Form Only) and Shape-Shift bonus action between true blob form and object form, with the Bite + Pseudopod attacks gaining auto-grapple + Disadvantage-on-escape-checks while in object form — composite of the existing Shape-Shift gap (Vampire / Mummy Lord / Lycanthropes / Hag / Genie family) with per-form action availability + an attached-condition-with-Disadvantage-modifier-on-escape escape-modifier primitive that doesn't exist today. (4) Phase Spider Ethereal Jaunt bonus action (teleport between Material and Ethereal Planes) and Ethereal Sight trait (60-ft cross-plane vision) — first cross-plane traversal mechanic in the pack; Night Hag's Etherealness from batch 4.11 is the closest existing analog but it's a long-duration spell-cast variant rather than a bonus-action toggle. Phase Spider Bite reduce-to-0-then-Stable-Poisoned-Paralyzed shares shape with the Vampire Familiar Umbral Dagger rider from batch 4.9. (5) Web Walker narrative (consumer territory; same shape as existing Giant Spider / Wolf Spider gap). Deferred under existing engine gaps: Multiattack on Death Dog + Phase Spider (Cockatrice, Mimic, Basilisk make single attacks per Attack action — three of the five Monstrosities in this batch are single-attack creatures, the densest single-batch cluster of single-attack-only Monstrosities); poison damage rider on attack (Basilisk Bite 2d6 poison + Phase Spider Bite 2d8 poison — both fold into the existing poison-damage-rider-on-attack gap with the Vrock / Hezrou / Spider cohort); recharge-4-6 area save on Basilisk Petrifying Gaze is the first non-recharge-5-6 / non-recharge-6 variant in the pack (every other Recharge action in the pack is recharge-5-6 or recharge-6), exercising the schema's `rechargeMin: 4` value for the first time outside its enum range proof; Mimic + Phase Spider Shape-Shift / Ethereal Jaunt fold into the existing Shape-Shift gap with bonus-action variants.

Mephit Recharge-6 area breaths (Dust Blinding Breath / Ice Frost Breath / Magma Fire Breath / Steam Steam Breath, all 15-ft cones — Blinding Breath alone is a non-damaging condition-on-fail variant and is the fifth user of the slice-4.5 secondary-non-damaging-breath gap; Djinni Storm Bolt's Large-or-smaller Prone-on-hit rider folds into the existing on-hit-save-or-condition gap (the closest matching shape is the Adult-Chromatic / Wereboar Charge prone rider, but applied as an attack-roll-on-hit rather than a save-or-condition save shape). Efreeti Hurl Flame (7d6 fire ranged attack) is the highest-flat-elemental-damage ranged attack in the pack for a non-Ancient-Dragon, matching the existing per-color elemental-ranged-attack riders. Djinni + Efreeti both carry the 30%-chance-of-Wish-spell-cast-on-behalf-of-another mechanic with 3/year usage cap and no stress to the genie (Wishes trait) — narrative / consumer territory under the wish gap. Djinni + Efreeti Elemental Restoration (1d4-day plane-respawn) is narrative analogous to Demonic / Diabolical Restoration. Mephit "Primordial (Auran / Aquan / Ignan / Terran)" subdialect specifications in the SRD language line are deferred to just "primordial" per the existing pack convention.

Sea + Green Hag both ship Amphibious (narrative; consumer territory). The three hags share four cross-cutting deferred mechanics: (1) Coven Magic (all three) — once-per-long-rest spell-cast-from-a-list (Augury / Find Familiar / Identify / Locate Object / Scrying / Unseen Servant) gated on "within 30 ft of at least two hag allies"; needs a coven-coordination predicate primitive + per-long-rest budget + spell-as-monster-action, none of which compose for a hag today, folds into the existing innate-spellcasting envelope gap with the additional ally-proximity predicate; (2) Vile Appearance (Sea Hag only) — start-of-turn aura DC 11 WIS save vs Frightened until start of target's next turn, with the 24-hour per-target immunity stamp shared shape with the Adult-Chromatic Frightful Presence / Mummy Dreadful Glare / Pit Fiend Fear Aura, restricted to Beast and Humanoid targets (a per-creature-type predicate gate the engine doesn't model today — the closest analogue is the Bite-vs-Humanoid restriction on lycanthrope curse riders); (3) Mimicry (Green Hag) — narrative animal-sound / humanoid-voice imitation with a perceive-as-imitation DC 14 WIS (Insight) check, consumer territory; (4) Death Glare (Sea Hag Recharge 5-6) — target Frightened creature within 30 ft, WIS DC 11 save fail vs instant-drop-to-0-HP-if-HP-is-20-or-less / 3d8 psychic damage otherwise; the instant-0-HP-at-or-below-threshold mechanic is the densest novel deferral in this batch (composite of a target-condition-predicate `frightened` + an HP-threshold-check + a binary-outcome resolution branching on HP — closest existing analog is Sea Hag's own Vile Appearance's per-target-immunity stamp, but the HP-threshold predicate is genuinely new). Night Hag-specific deferrals: Soul Bag — a per-statblock attached-stateful-item-as-entity with AC 15 / HP 20 / resistance-to-all-damage + 7-day-recreation-after-destruction lifecycle (extends the Erinyes Magic Rope gap from batch 4.2 with additional HP / resistance / recreation parameters and a "while-holding-bag" trigger predicate gating an entire action like Nightmare Haunting); Nightmare Haunting (1/Day, requires Soul Bag) — Dream-spell-as-monster-action with on-Ethereal-Plane gating, a creature-Hit-Point-maximum-decrease-on-Dream-damage rider, a soul-trap-on-kill rider, and a no-raise-from-dead-until-soul-released ghost-state rider — composite of spell-as-monster-action + cross-plane gating + multiple novel kill-state riders, the densest novel deferral surfaced this batch; Etherealness at-will spell, Phantasmal Killer 2/Day, Plane Shift 2/Day (self-only) and Magic Missile L4 at-will all fold into the innate-spellcasting envelope gap; Shape-Shift bonus action (Small or Medium Humanoid form, same stats other than size) folds into the Vampire / lycanthrope Shape-Shift gap. Green Hag-specific deferrals: Spellcasting envelope (Dancing Lights + Disguise Self + Invisibility + Minor Illusion + Ray of Sickness L3 at-will, all using WIS as spellcasting ability — first Hag innate-spellcasting list with non-INT spellcasting ability, exercising the spell-ability-key parameterization of the existing innate-spellcasting envelope); Disguise Self with a 24-hour duration (vs RAW base of 1 hour) and Invisibility with a "leaves no tracks" qualifier are two narrow parameterizations of existing spells. All three hags share the on-hit-Poison damage rider on Claw shape: Green Hag's Claw adds 1d6 poison damage on hit (folds into the existing on-hit-poison-damage-rider gap shared with Giant Spider / Wolf Spider / Ankheg / Bone Devil / Assassin / Vrock / Hezrou); Sea + Night Hag Claws have no rider, exercising the absence-of-rider case. Pack monster total moves from 161 to 164. Fey type roster moves from 4 to 6 (Sea + Green Hag); Fiend type roster moves from 16 to 17 (Night Hag).

**Lycanthrope cohort, content-monsters-batch-4.10 (5):** Wererat (CR 2), Werewolf (CR 3), Wereboar (CR 4), Weretiger (CR 4), Werebear (CR 5). Closes the canonical SRD 5.2.1 Lycanthrope roster in a single batch (every SRD 5.2.1 lycanthrope now ships) and adds the first explicit `subtype: "lycanthrope"` cohort to the pack. Every entry sourced verbatim from `references/srd-markdown/monsters-A-Z.md` on AC / HP / abilities / saves / damage R/I/V / condition immunities / senses / CR / XP / PB. The cohort opens a tight alignment span: NG (Werebear), NE (Wereboar), LE (Wererat), CE (Werewolf), N (Weretiger) — Werebear is the first NG monster in the pack and Weretiger the first true-neutral entry that isn't an unaligned beast. (bear form only)"; Wereboar: "30 ft., 40 ft. (boar form only)"). The pack ships true-form speeds only (the schema is a single statblock per entry, no form variants); form-specific bonuses (Werebear's bear-form 40-walk + climb 30, Wereboar / Weretiger / Werewolf's animal-form 40-walk) are deferred under the existing Vampire-style Shape-Shift gap. Deferred under existing engine gaps: Multiattack on all five (with the "multiattack-with-options" pattern — every lycanthrope makes two of one weapon attack with a one-attack Bite/Gore swap-in, the same shape exercised by the Metallic Dragon Multiattack-with-secondary-breath gap); Shape-Shift bonus action across all five (folds into the Vampire Shape-Shift gap with a per-tier-statblock-name swap — Werebear true/hybrid/bear, Wereboar true/hybrid/boar etc — and a per-form action availability restriction shared with Vampire's "Vampire Form Only" / "Bat or Vampire Form Only" labels on Multiattack + Grave Strike + Bite); Lycanthropy curse rider on Bite (Werebear) / Gore (Wereboar) / Bite (Wererat) / Bite (Weretiger) / Bite (Werewolf) — every lycanthrope's signature attack carries a CON save (DC 11-14 per CR tier) vs cursed-on-drop-to-0-turn-into-the-same-lycanthrope-under-GM-control with a 24-hour per-target immunity stamp on save success. This is the densest single primitive deferral surfacing in this batch: composite of on-hit-save-or-condition + on-drop-to-0-spawn-specific-statblock-with-set-HP + the per-target 24-hour immunity stamp (shared shape with Mummy Dreadful Glare + Adult Chromatic Frightful Presence) + the GM-control variant of the existing summon-spawn primitive family. The five distinct DCs (DC 11 / 12 / 12 / 13 / 14 for Wererat / Wereboar / Werewolf / Weretiger / Werebear) exercise the per-tier DC scaling first introduced by the Chromatic / Metallic dragon breath weapons. Wereboar Tusk Charge (movement-conditional 2d6 extra damage + Prone if moved 20+ feet straight before hit) folds into the existing Charge gap (Boar / Minotaur / Unicorn / Allosaurus / Triceratops / Mammoth). Werewolf Pack Tactics (advantage on attack rolls when an ally is within 5 feet of target) joins the existing Pack Tactics gap (Tough / Warrior Infantry / Giant Rat / Dire Wolf — five users now). Weretiger Prowl bonus action (move up to speed without provoking Opportunity Attacks + Hide at end of movement) is a novel mechanic-level deferral: composite of an opportunity-attack-immunity-for-this-movement primitive (similar in shape to the Pteranodon Flyby gap but covering all directions rather than only flight-away) plus an inline Hide-action chain. Werebear / Wereboar / Wererat / Weretiger / Werewolf all carry typed weapon Gear (4 Handaxes / 6 Javelins / Hand Crossbow / Longbow / Longbow) referenced by their Multiattack actions, exercising the existing pack convention of associating weapon-Gear with the monster's Multiattack chain rather than carrying typed item-instances per statblock. The five animal forms (bear / boar / rat / tiger / wolf) all already ship in the pack as standalone Beast statblocks (Brown Bear, Boar, Giant Rat, Mastiff-as-proxy, Wolf) — when monster Shape-Shift wires, the lycanthrope-form-into-existing-Beast-statblock route is the cleanest install path, avoiding a per-lycanthrope form duplicate.
